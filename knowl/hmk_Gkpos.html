Exercise 3.

Assume \(k \gt 0\) and let \(s = 1/\sqrt{k}\text{.}\) Derive the following measurement formulas in \((\mathbb{P}^2_k,{\cal S}_k)\text{.}\)

  1. The length of a line segment from \(0\) to \(x\text{,}\) where \(0 \lt x \leq s\) is
    \begin{equation*} d_{k}(0,x) = 2s\arctan(x/s)\text{.} \end{equation*}
  2. The circumference of the circle centered at the origin with elliptic radius \(r \lt \pi/(2\sqrt{k})\) is \(C=2\pi s\sin(r/s)\text{.}\)
  3. The area of the circle centered at the origin with elliptic radius \(r \lt \pi/(2\sqrt{k})\) is \(\displaystyle A = 4\pi s^2 \sin^2\left(\frac{r}{2s}\right)\text{.}\)
