First translate the line \(x = k\) to the \(y\)-axis, then rotate by \(-\pi/2\text{,}\) sending the \(y\)-axis to the \(x\)-axis. Then reflect across the \(x\)-axis, rotate back, and translate back:

\begin{align*} z \amp\mapsto z - k \amp \text{(translate)} \\ \amp\mapsto e^{-i\pi/2}(z-k) \amp \text{(rotate)}\\ \amp\mapsto \overline{e^{-i\pi/2}(z-k)} \amp \text{(reflect)}\\ \amp= e^{i\pi/2}(\overline{z}-k) \\ \amp\mapsto e^{i\pi/2}e^{i\pi/2}(\overline{z}-k) \amp \text{(rotate back)}\\ \amp=-(\overline{z}-k) \amp (e^{i\pi} = -1)\\ \amp\mapsto -(\overline{z}-k) + k \amp \text{(translate back)} \\ \amp= 2k - \overline{z}\text{.} \end{align*}

Thus reflection about the vertical line \(x = k\) is given by \(T(z) = 2k - \overline{z}\text{.}\)
