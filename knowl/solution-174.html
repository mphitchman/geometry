Solution 7.3.5.1.

Suppose \(z\) is at the origin, the hyperbolic line \(L\) is perpendicular to the positive real axis, and it intersects the real axis at \(x\) where \(0 \lt x \lt s = \frac{1}{\sqrt{|k|}}\text{.}\) In this case Lobatchevsky's formula is

\begin{align*} \tan\left(\frac{\theta}{2}\right) \amp= e^{-\sqrt{|k|}d}\\ \amp= e^{-\sqrt{|k|}s\ln\left(\frac{s+x}{s-x}\right)}\\ \amp= \frac{s-x}{s+x}\\ \amp= \frac{1 - \sqrt{|k|}x}{1+\sqrt{|k|}}\text{.} \end{align*}

As \(k\) approaches 0 this expression approaches 1, regardless of the value of \(d\text{.}\) Then \(\tan(\theta/2)\) approaches 1 so that \(\theta\) approaches \(\pi/2\text{,}\) independent of the value of \(d\text{.}\)
