Definition 6.3.1.

If \(\boldsymbol{r}: [a,b] \to \mathbb{P}^2\) is a smooth curve in \((\mathbb{P}^2,{\cal S})\text{,}\) the length of \(\boldsymbol{r}\), denoted \({\cal L}(\boldsymbol{r})\text{,}\) is given by

\begin{equation*} {\cal L}(\boldsymbol{r}) = \int_a^b \frac{2|\boldsymbol{r}^\prime(t)|}{1 + |\boldsymbol{r}(t)|^2}~dt. \end{equation*}

The area of a figure \(R\) described in polar coordinates in the elliptic space \(\mathbb{P}^2\text{,}\) denoted \(A(R)\text{,}\) is given by

\begin{equation*} A(R) = \iint_R \frac{4r}{(1+r^2)^2}dr d\theta\text{.} \end{equation*}
