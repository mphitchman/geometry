Exercise 12.

In this exercise we prove the hyperbolic law of sines. We assume our triangle is as in FigureÂ 5.4.24. Thus, \(q = ke^{i\gamma}\) for some \(0 \lt k \lt 1\) and \(p = r\) for some real number \(0 \lt r \lt 1\text{.}\) Suppose further that the circle containing side \(c\) has center \(z_0\) and Euclidean radius \(R\text{,}\) shown in the figure, and that \(m_q\) is the midpoint of segment \(qq^*\) and \(m_p\) is the midpoint of segment \(pp^*\text{,}\) so that \(\Delta z_0m_qq\) and \(\Delta pm_pz_0\) are right triangles.

  1. Verify that the triangle angles \(\alpha\) and \(\beta\) correspond to angles \(\angle m_qz_0q\) and \(\angle pz_0m_p\text{,}\) respectively.
  2. Notice that \(\sin(\alpha) = |m_q-q|/R\) and \(\sin(\beta) = |m_p-p|/R.\) Verify that \(|m_q-q| = \frac{1/k - k}{2} = \frac{1 - k^2}{2k}\) and that \(|m_p-p| = \frac{1-r^2}{2r}\text{.}\)
  3. Verify that
    \begin{equation*} \frac{\sinh(a)}{\sin(\alpha)} = \frac{\sinh(b)}{\sin(\beta)}\text{.} \end{equation*}
  4. Explain why we may conclude that for any hyperbolic triangle in \(\mathbb{D}\text{,}\)
    \begin{equation*} \frac{\sinh(a)}{\sin(\alpha)} = \frac{\sinh(b)}{\sin(\beta)}=\frac{\sinh(c)}{\sin(\gamma)}\text{.} \end{equation*}
