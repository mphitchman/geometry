Assume the circle with hyperbolic radius \(r\) is centered at the origin and the Euclidean radius of the circle is \(a\text{,}\) where

\begin{equation*} a = \frac{e^r - 1}{e^r + 1}\text{.} \end{equation*}

As a planar curve, the circle is parameterized by

\begin{equation*} \boldsymbol{r}(t) = a \cos(t) + a \sin(t) i \end{equation*}

for \(0 \leq t \leq 2\pi\text{.}\) The circumference of the circle is then

\begin{equation*} {\cal L}(t) = \int_0^{2\pi} \frac{2|\boldsymbol{r}^\prime(t)|}{1-|\boldsymbol{r}(t)|^2}~dt\text{.} \end{equation*}

One checks that \(|\boldsymbol{r}^\prime(t)| = |\boldsymbol{r}(t)| = a\) so that

\begin{equation*} {\cal L}(t) = \int_0^{2\pi} \frac{2a}{1-a^2}~dt = 4\pi \frac{a}{1-a^2}\text{.} \end{equation*}

Finally, express this circumference in terms of the hyperbolic radius, \(r\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} 4\pi \frac{a}{1-a^2} \amp= 4\pi\frac{\frac{e^r - 1}{e^r + 1}}{1-\frac{(e^r - 1)^2}{(e^r + 1)^2}}\\ \amp= 4\pi\frac{(e^r-1)(e^r+1)}{(e^r+1)^2-(e^r-1)^2}\\ \amp= 4\pi\frac{e^{2r}- 1}{(e^{2r}+2e^r+1)-(e^{2r}-2e^r+1)}\\ \amp= 4\pi\frac{e^{2r}-1}{4e^r}\\ \amp= 2\pi\frac{e^r - e^{-r}}{2}\\ \amp= 2\pi\sinh(r)\text{.} \end{align*}
