The solution below follows the solution appearing in [1]. We introduce some short hand notation. Let \(A = \cosh(a)\text{,}\) \(B = \cosh(b)\) and \(C = \cosh(c)\text{.}\) By ExerciseÂ 5.4.1 (c), it follows that \(\sinh(a) = \sqrt{A^2 - 1}\text{.}\) Similarly, \(\sinh(b) = \sqrt{B^2 - 1}\) and \(\sinh(c) = \sqrt{C^2 - 1}.\) Now, we set about expressing each term of the right hand side of the second hyperbolic law of cosines in terms of \(A, B,\) and \(C\text{.}\)

By the first hyperbolic law of cosines, we can express

\begin{equation*} \cos(\gamma) = \frac{AB - C}{\sqrt{A^2 - 1}\sqrt{B^2 - 1}}\text{.} \end{equation*}

Applying the first law of cosines to the other two angles yields analogous formulas:

\begin{equation*} \cos(\alpha) = \frac{BC-A}{\sqrt{B^2 - 1}\sqrt{C^2 - 1}} \text{ and } \cos(\beta) = \frac{AC-B}{\sqrt{A^2 - 1}\sqrt{C^2 - 1}}\text{.} \end{equation*}

Furthermore,

\begin{align*} \sin(\alpha)\sin(\beta) \amp= \sqrt{1-\cos^2(\alpha)}\cdot\sqrt{1-\cos^2(\beta)} \\ \amp= \sqrt{1-\frac{(BC-A)^2}{(B^2 - 1)(C^2 - 1)}}\cdot \sqrt{1-\frac{(AC-B)^2}{(A^2 - 1)(C^2 - 1)}}\\ \amp= \sqrt{\frac{[(B^2 - 1)(C^2 - 1)-(BC-A)^2]\cdot[(A^2-1)(C^2-1)-(AC-B)^2]}{(A^2-1)(B^2-1)(C^2-1)^2}}\\ \amp= \frac{1 - A^2 - B^2 - C^2 + 2ABC}{\sqrt{(A^2-1)(B^2-1)(C^2-1)^2}}\text{.} \end{align*}

Putting these pieces together,

\begin{align*} \frac{\cos(\alpha)\cos(\beta)+\cos(\gamma)}{\sin(\alpha)\sin(\beta)}\amp= \frac{\frac{BC-A}{\sqrt{B^2 - 1}\sqrt{C^2 - 1}}\cdot\frac{AC-B}{\sqrt{A^2 - 1}\sqrt{C^2 - 1}}+\frac{AB - C}{\sqrt{A^2 - 1}\sqrt{B^2 - 1}}}{\frac{1 - A^2 - B^2 - C^2 + 2ABC}{\sqrt{(A^2-1)(B^2-1)(C^2-1)^2}}}\\ \amp= \frac{\frac{(BC-A)(AC-B)}{\sqrt{(A^2-1)(B^2 - 1)(C^2-1)^2}}+\frac{(AB - C)(C^2-1)}{\sqrt{(A^2 - 1)(B^2 - 1)(C^2-1)^2}}}{\frac{1 - A^2 - B^2 - C^2 + 2ABC}{\sqrt{(A^2-1)(B^2-1)(C^2-1)^2}}}\\ \amp=\frac{(BC-A)(AC-B)+(AB-C)(C^2-1)}{1-A^2-B^2-C^2 + 2ABC}\\ \amp= \frac{ABC^2 - A^2C-B^2C+AB+ABC^2-C^3-AB+C}{1-A^2-B^2-C^2 + 2ABC}\\ \amp= \frac{C(1-A^2-B^2-C^2+2ABC)}{1-A^2-B^2-C^2 + 2ABC}\\ \amp= C\text{.} \end{align*}
