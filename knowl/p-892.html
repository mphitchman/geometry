The distance

\begin{equation*} d_H(0,x) = \ln\left(\frac{1+x}{1-x}\right) = k\text{.} \end{equation*}

So

\begin{equation*} \frac{1+x}{1-x} = e^k \end{equation*}

and solving for \(x\text{:}\)

\begin{align*} 1 + x \amp= e^k - xe^k \\ x + xe^k \amp= e^k - 1 \\ x \amp= \frac{e^k - 1}{e^k + 1}\text{.} \end{align*}

Plug in \(k = 2003007.4\) to find the value of \(x\) on the positive real axis that produces a hyperbolic segment (\(0x\)) of length \(k\text{.}\)
