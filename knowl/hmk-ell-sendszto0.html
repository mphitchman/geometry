Exercise 1.

The transformation group in elliptic geometry.

  1. Prove that \(\cal{S}\) is a group of transformations.
  2. For each \(\theta \in \mathbb{R}\) and \(z_0 \in \mathbb{C}\text{,}\) prove that the following Möbius transformation is in \(\cal S\text{:}\)
    \begin{equation*} T(z)=e^{i\theta}\frac{z-z_0}{1+\overline{z_0}z}\text{.} \end{equation*}
  3. For each \(\theta \in \mathbb{R}\text{,}\) prove that \(T(z) = e^{i\theta}\frac{1}{z}\) is in \(\cal S\text{.}\)
  4. Use (b) and (c) to prove that for any distinct points \(p, q \in \mathbb{C}^+\) there exists a transformation in \(\cal S\) that sends \(p\) to 0 and \(q\) to a point on the positive real axis, thus proving Lemma 6.2.3.
