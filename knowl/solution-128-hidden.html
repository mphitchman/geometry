We express \(d_H(r,re^{2\pi i/n})\) in terms of \(r\) and \(n\) with the working formula for hyperbolic distance. For convenience, set \(\alpha = \frac{2\pi}{n}\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} d_H(r,re^{i \alpha}) \amp= \ln\bigg(\frac{|1 - r^2e^{i\alpha}|+|re^{i \alpha} - r|}{|1 - r^2e^{i\alpha}|-|re^{i \alpha} - r|}\bigg)\\ \amp= \ln\bigg(\frac{|1 - r^2\cos(\alpha)-r^2\sin(\alpha)i|+r|\cos(\alpha) - 1+\sin(\alpha)i|}{|1 - r^2\cos(\alpha)-r^2\sin(\alpha)i|-r|\cos(\alpha) - 1+\sin(\alpha)i|}\bigg)\\ \amp= \ln\bigg(\frac{\sqrt{(1-r^2\cos(\alpha))^2 + (r^2\sin(\alpha))^2}+r\sqrt{(\cos(\alpha)-1)^2 + \sin^2(\alpha)}}{\sqrt{(1-r^2\cos(\alpha))^2 + (r^2\sin(\alpha))^2}-r\sqrt{(\cos(\alpha)-1)^2 + \sin^2(\alpha)}}\bigg)\\ \amp= \ln\bigg(\frac{\sqrt{1-2r^2\cos(\alpha)+r^4}+r\sqrt{2-2\cos(\alpha)}} {\sqrt{1-2r^2\cos(\alpha)+r^4}-r\sqrt{2-2\cos(\alpha)}}\bigg) \end{align*}

Multiplying top and bottom by the conjugate, the expression simplifies to

\begin{align*} \amp~~ \ln\bigg(\frac{(1-2r^2\cos(\alpha)+r^4)+2r\sqrt{1-2r^2\cos(\alpha)+r^4}\sqrt{2-2\cos(\alpha)}+r^2(2-2\cos(\alpha))} {(1-2r^2\cos(\alpha)+r^4)-r^2(2-2\cos(\alpha))}\bigg)\\ \amp= \ln\bigg(\frac{1 +2r^2 + r^4 - 4r^2\cos(\alpha) + 2r\sqrt{[1-2r^2\cos(\alpha)+r^4][2-2\cos(\alpha)]}}{1 - 2r^2 + r^4}\bigg)\\ \amp= \ln\bigg(\frac{(1+r^2)^2 - 4r^2\cos(\alpha)+2r\sqrt{[1-2r^2\cos(\alpha)+r^4][2-2\cos(\alpha)]}}{(1-r^2)^2}\bigg) \end{align*}

Replacing \(\alpha\) with \(\frac{2\pi}{n}\) and the distance is expressed in terms of \(r\) and \(n\text{,}\) though it's not too pretty.
