Item 3.5.9.b.
If \(T\) is hyperbolic, \(T\) has normal form
\begin{equation*} \frac{T(z)-p}{T(z)-q} = r\frac{z-p}{z-q} \end{equation*}
and letting \(z = z_\infty\) we have
\begin{equation*} 1 = r\frac{z_\infty-p}{z_\infty-q} \end{equation*}
which implies \(z_\infty - q = r(z_\infty - p)\text{.}\) That is, as vectors, \(z_\infty - q\) and \(z_\infty - p\) are parallel. Thus, \(z_\infty\) is on the line through \(p\) and \(q\text{.}\) It follows that \(w_\infty\) is on this line as well, by LemmaÂ 3.5.7.
