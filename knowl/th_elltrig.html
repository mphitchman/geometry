Theorem 6.3.14.

Suppose we have an elliptic triangle with elliptic side lengths \(a,b,\) and \(c\text{,}\) and angles \(\alpha, \beta\text{,}\) and \(\gamma\) as in FigureÂ 6.3.13. Then the following laws hold:

  1. Elliptic law of cosines
    \begin{equation*} \cos(c)=\cos(a)\cos(b)+\sin(a)\sin(b)\cos(\gamma)\text{.} \end{equation*}
  2. Elliptic law of sines
    \begin{equation*} \frac{\sin(a)}{\sin(\alpha)}=\frac{\sin(b)}{\sin(\beta)}=\frac{\sin(c)}{\sin(\gamma)}\text{.} \end{equation*}
