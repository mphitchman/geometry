The sphere with constant curvature \(k\) has radius equal to \(1/\sqrt{k}\text{,}\) and area equal to \(4\pi/k\text{.}\) Since the sphere has Euler characteristic 2, the Gauss-Bonnet formula holds in this case. The projective plane \(\mathbb{P}^2\) with curvature \(k\) has area equal to \(2\pi/k\text{,}\) and Euler characteristic equal to 1, so the result holds in this case as well. The torus and the Klein bottle each have \(k = 0\) and \(\chi = 0\text{,}\) so in this case the Gauss-Bonnet formula reduces to the true statement that \(0 = 0\text{.}\)
