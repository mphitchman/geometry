  1. If \(\boldsymbol{r}(t) = x(t) + i y(t)\) is a smooth curve in \(\mathbb{D}\) its image under reflection about the \(x\)-axis is \(\overline{\boldsymbol{r}(t)} = x(t) - i y(t)\text{,}\) and \(|\boldsymbol{r}(t)| = |\overline{\boldsymbol{r}(t)}|\text{.}\) Moreover, \(\overline{\boldsymbol{r}^\prime(t)} = x^\prime(t) - i y^\prime(t)\) so \(|\boldsymbol{r}^\prime(t)| = |\overline{\boldsymbol{r}^\prime(t)}|\) and it follows that the the length of \(\boldsymbol{r}\) is equal to the length of \(\overline{\boldsymbol{r}}\text{.}\)
  2. Consider a hyperbolic line \(L\) and a smooth curve \(\boldsymbol{r}\) in \(\mathbb{D}\text{.}\) Let \(\overline{\boldsymbol{r}}\) denote the inversion of \(\boldsymbol{r}\) about \(L\text{.}\) We want \({\cal L}(\boldsymbol{r}) = {\cal L}(\overline{\boldsymbol{r}})\text{.}\) There exists a map \(T\) in \(\cal H\) that sends \(L\) to the real axis. Since \(T\) is a Möbius transformation it preserves symmetry points, so \(T(\overline{\boldsymbol{r}})\) is reflection of \(T(\boldsymbol{r})\) across the real axis. By part (a),
    \begin{equation*} {\cal L}(T(\boldsymbol{r})) = {\cal L}(T(\overline{\boldsymbol{r}})) \end{equation*}
    and by Theorem 5.3.7
    \begin{equation*} {\cal L}(\boldsymbol{r}) = {\cal L}(\overline{\boldsymbol{r}})\text{.} \end{equation*}
