Suppose \(\beta\) and \(\gamma\) are two points having the same distance from \(u\text{,}\) the same distance from \(v\text{,}\) and the same distance from \(w\text{.}\) That is, \(|u-\beta| = |u-\gamma|\) and \(|v - \beta | = |v - \gamma|\) and \(|w-\beta| = |w-\gamma|\text{.}\) By TheoremÂ 2.4.2 all points \(z\) satisfying the equation \(|z - \beta| = |z - \gamma|\) determine a line if \(\beta \neq \gamma\text{.}\) Since \(u\text{,}\) \(v\) and \(w\) all satisfy the equation, and they do not all lie on a single line, then \(\beta\) and \(\gamma\) cannot be distinct. Thus, the position of a point is uniquely determined by its distance from 3 non-collinear points.
