Exercise 10

Prove that any pair of nonintersecting clines in \(\mathbb{C}\) may be mapped by a Möbius transformation to concentric circles. Hint: By Theorem 3.2.16 there are two points \(p\) and \(q\) in \(\mathbb{C}\) that are symmetric with respect to both clines. What happens if we apply a Möbius transformation that takes one of these points to \(\infty\text{?}\)
