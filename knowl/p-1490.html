Consider the following diagram TODO of the 10-gon representation of \(C_5\text{.}\) Start travelling clockwise around the corner between the \(a_1\) edges. You will find that you traverse the corners in a consecutive fashion, heading clockwise around the 10-gon, as indicated by the numbering of the corners in the accompanying diagram. When all 10 corners have been traversed, you will return to your initial starting point. Thus all 10 corners come together at a single point. This progression holds for all \(g\text{,}\) not just \(g=5\text{.}\) Given the \(2g\)-gon (with \(g \geq 1\)) having boundary label \(a_1a_1\cdots a_ga_g\text{,}\) if one begins a tour of the corners by travelling clockwise around the corner between the \(a_1\) edges, one will always traverse all corners in a clockwise progression before returning to the starting point.
