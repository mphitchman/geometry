FigureÂ 5.1.7(b) depicts the image of \(M\) under two applications of a translation of the hyperbolic plane. The translation is generated by two inversions about non-intersecting clines \(L_1\) and \(L_2\) (that meet \(\mathbb{S}^1_\infty\) at right angles). The fixed points of the map are the points \(p\) and \(q\) on the circle at infinity. Under this translation, any point \(z\) in \(\mathbb{D}\) heads away from \(p\) and toward \(q\) on the unique cline through the three points \(p,q,\) and \(z\text{.}\)
