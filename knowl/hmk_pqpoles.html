Exercise 9.

Suppose the Möbius transformation \(T\) fixes the distinct, finite points \(p\) and \(q\) and sends \(z_\infty\) to \(\infty\) and \(\infty\) to \(w_\infty\text{.}\) By Lemma 3.5.7 we know \(p+q=z_\infty+w_\infty\text{.}\) Use the normal form of \(T\) to prove the following facts.

  1. If \(T\) is elliptic then the four points \(p\text{,}\) \(q\text{,}\) \(z_\infty\text{,}\) and \(w_\infty\) form a rhombus. Under what conditions is this rhombus actually a square?
  2. If \(T\) is hyperbolic then these 4 points all lie on the same Euclidean line.
  3. If \(T\) is loxodromic, then under what conditions do these four points determine a rectangle?
