This circular region may be described in polar coordinates by \(0 \leq \theta \leq 2\pi\) and \(0 \leq r \leq x\text{.}\) The area of the region is then given by the following integral, which we compute with the \(u\)-substitution \(u = 1 - r^2\text{:}\)

\begin{align*} \amp \int_0^{2\pi} \int_0^x \frac{4r}{(1-r^2)^2}~drd\theta\\ \amp = \left(\int_0^{2\pi}~d\theta\right)\left(\int_1^{1-x^2}\frac{-2}{u^2}~du\right)\\ \amp = 2\pi\left[\frac{2}{u} \bigg|_1^{1-x^2}\right]\\ \amp = 2\pi \left[ \frac{2}{1-x^2} - 2\right]\\ \amp = 4\pi \frac{x^2}{1-x^2}\text{.} \end{align*}
