Solution 6.2.1.1.

  1. \(\cal S\) consists of all Möbius transformations that preserve antipodal points. The identity Möbius transformation fixes every point, so it preserves antipodal points and is in \(\cal S\text{.}\)

    If \(T\) and \(S\) are two Möbius transformations that preserve antipodal points, then \(T \circ S\) is also a Möbius transformation, and it preserves antipodal points as well: if \(z\) and \(z_a\) are antipodal, then \(S(z)\) and \(S(z_a)\) are antipodal since \(S\) preserves antipodal points; and then \(T(S(z))\) and \(T(S(z_a))\) are antipodal since \(T\) preserves antipodal points. Thus, if \(T\) and \(S\) are in \(\cal S\) then so is \(T \circ S\text{.}\)

    Finally, if \(T\) is in \(\cal S\text{,}\) then \(T^{-1}\) is a Möbius transformation because Möbius transformations form a group of transformations. To see that \(T^{-1}\) preserves antipodal points, consider an arbitrary pair of antipodal points in \(\mathbb{C}^+\text{,}\) say \(z\) and \(z_a\text{.}\) Let \(u = T^{-1}(z)\) and \(v = T^{-1}(z_a)\text{.}\) We claim that \(u\) and \(v\) are antipodal points. Indeed, \(T\) preserves antipodal points and \(T(u) = z\text{,}\) so \(T(u_a) = z_a\) where \(u_a\) is the point antipodal to \(u\text{.}\) But \(T(v) = z_a\) as well, and since \(T\) is one-to-one, it follows that \(v = u_a\) and \(T^{-1}\) is also in \(\cal S\text{.}\)

  2. Suppose \(z\) and \(z_a\) are antipodal points and
    \begin{equation*} T(z) = e^{i\theta}\frac{z - z_0}{1 + \overline{z_0}z}\text{.} \end{equation*}
    We show \(T(z)\) and \(T(z_a)\) are antipodal.
    \begin{align*} T(z)\overline{T(z_a)} \amp= e^{i\theta}\frac{z - z_0}{1 + \overline{z_0}z}\cdot {e^{-i\theta}\frac{\overline{z_a} - \overline{z_0}}{1 + z_0\overline{z_a}}}\\ \amp= \frac{(z-z_0)(\overline{z_a} - \overline{z_0})}{(1+\overline{z_0}z)(1 + z_0\overline{z_a})}\\ \amp= \frac{z\overline{z_a}(z-z_0)(\overline{z_a} - \overline{z_0})}{z\overline{z_a}(1+\overline{z_0}z)(1 + z_0\overline{z_a})}\\ \amp= \frac{z\overline{z_a}(z-z_0)(\overline{z_a} - \overline{z_0})}{(\overline{z_a}+\overline{z_0}z\overline{z_a})(z + z_0z\overline{z_a})}\\ \amp=\frac{-(z-z_0)(\overline{z_a} - \overline{z_0})}{(\overline{z_a}-\overline{z_0})(z - z_0)}\tag{$z\overline{z_a} = -1$}\\ \amp= -1\text{.} \end{align*}
