Solution 7.1.1.1.

The curvature of the projective plane is

\begin{align*} k\amp= -3 \lim_{r \to 0^+} \frac{d^2}{dr^2}\left[\frac{2\pi\sin(r)}{2\pi r}\right]\\ \amp= -3 \lim_{r \to 0^+}\frac{d^2}{dr^2}\left[\frac{r - \frac{r^3}{3!}+\frac{r^5}{5!}-\cdots}{r}\right]\\ \amp= -3 \lim_{r \to 0^+}\frac{d^2}{dr^2}\left[1 - \frac{r^2}{3!}+\frac{r^4}{5!} - \frac{r^6}{7!}+\cdots\right]\\ \amp=-3 \lim_{r \to 0^+}\frac{d}{dr}\left[-\frac{2r}{3!}+\frac{4r^3}{5!} - \frac{6r^5}{7!}+\cdots\right]\\ \amp= -3\lim_{r \to 0^+} \left[-\frac{1}{3}+\frac{r^2}{5\cdot 2} - \frac{r^4}{7\cdot 4!}+\cdots\right]\\ \amp= -3 \cdot \left[-\frac{1}{3} + 0\right]\text{.} \end{align*}

Each term in the second derivative of the power expansion after the \(-1/3\) term has an \(r\) in the numerator and so is zero in the limit. Thus, the curvature of the projective plane is \(k = 1\text{.}\)
