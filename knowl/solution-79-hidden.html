Check that \(T\) has a single fixed point at \(z = 3\text{,}\) so we have the normal form

\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{T(z)-3} = \frac{1}{z-3}+d\text{.} \end{equation*}

Since \(T(\infty) = \frac{1+3i}{i} = 3-i\) it follows that \(d = i\) so that the normal form is

\begin{equation*} T(z) = \frac{1}{T(z)-3} = \frac{1}{z-3}+i \text{.} \end{equation*}

The single line of motion in the system of tangent clines goes through 3 and \(p+1/d = 3-i\text{;}\) it is vertical. Each circle in our system has center on the real axis.
