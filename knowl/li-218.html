Item 5.4.12.a.
Since segment \(z_0q\) is a radius of the circle, this segment meets the line tangent to the circle at \(q\) at an angle of \(\pi/2\text{.}\) It follows that \(\angle z_0qm_q + \pi/2 + \alpha = \pi\text{,}\) so that \(\angle z_0qm_q = \pi/2 - \alpha\text{.}\) Then, by the right triangle \(\Delta qz_0m_q\) it follows that \(\angle m_qz_0q = \alpha\text{.}\) A similar argument applies for \(\angle pz_0m_p\text{.}\)
