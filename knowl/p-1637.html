Check directly:

\begin{align*} \phi(P)\phi(-P)^* \amp= \bigg(\frac{a}{1-d}\textbf{i}+\frac{b}{1-d}\textbf{j}+\frac{c}{1-d}{\bf k}\bigg)\bigg(-\frac{-a}{1+d}\textbf{i}-\frac{-b}{1+d}{\bf j}-\frac{-c}{1+d}\textbf{k}\bigg)\\ \amp= -\bigg(\frac{a}{1-d}\bigg)\bigg(\frac{a}{1+d}\bigg) -\bigg(\frac{b}{1-d}\bigg)\bigg(\frac{b}{1+d}\bigg) -\bigg(\frac{c}{1-d}\bigg)\bigg(\frac{c}{1+d}\bigg)+\text{junk}\text{,} \end{align*}

where the junk consists of cross terms that all cancel in pairs. It follows that

\begin{equation*} \phi(P)\phi(-P)^* = -\frac{a^2 + b^2 + c^2}{1-d^2}\text{.} \end{equation*}

Since \(P\) is on the 3-sphere, \(a^2 + b^2 + c^2 + d^2 = 1\text{,}\) so

\begin{equation*} \phi(P)\phi(-P)^* = -1\text{.} \end{equation*}
