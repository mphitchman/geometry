Solution 3.5.9.1.
  1. From the previous exercise we know that \(z_\infty\) and \(w_\infty\) lie on the perpendicular bisector of segment \(pq\text{.}\) Moreover, reflection across line \(pq\) takes \(z_\infty\) to \(w_\infty\text{,}\) so it follows that the four points form a rhombus. We obtain a square if \(\theta = \pi/2\) or \(3\pi/2\text{,}\) where \(\lambda = e^{i\theta}\) is the constant in the normal form expression of the elliptic map.
  2. If \(T\) is hyperbolic, \(T\) has normal form
    \begin{equation*} \frac{T(z)-p}{T(z)-q} = r\frac{z-p}{z-q} \end{equation*}
    and letting \(z = z_\infty\) we have
    \begin{equation*} 1 = r\frac{z_\infty-p}{z_\infty-q} \end{equation*}
    which implies \(z_\infty - q = r(z_\infty - p)\text{.}\) That is, as vectors, \(z_\infty - q\) and \(z_\infty - p\) are parallel. Thus, \(z_\infty\) is on the line through \(p\) and \(q\text{.}\) It follows that \(w_\infty\) is on this line as well, by Lemma 3.5.7.
  3. If \(T\) is loxodromic, \(T\) has normal form
    \begin{equation*} \frac{T(z)-p}{T(z)-q} = re^{i\theta}\frac{z-p}{z-q} \end{equation*}
    and letting \(z = z_\infty\) we have
    \begin{equation*} z_\infty-q = re^{i\theta}(z_\infty-p)\text{.} \end{equation*}
    The vectors \(z_\infty - q\) and \(z_\infty - p\) are perpendicular if \(\theta = \pi/2\) or \(3\pi/2\text{.}\) In this case, by Lemma 3.5.7, it follows that the four points \(p,q,z_\infty,w_\infty\) form a rectangle.
