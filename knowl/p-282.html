Since general linear transformations preserve lines, \(T(L_i)\) is the line through \(T(z_0)\) and \(T(z_i)\) for \(i = 1,2\) and it follows that

\begin{align*} \angle(T(L_1),T(L_2)) \amp = \arg\bigg(\frac{T(z_2)-T(z_0)}{T(z_1)-T(z_0)}\bigg)\\ \amp = \arg\bigg(\frac{az_2+b-az_0-b}{az_1+b-az_0-b}\bigg)\\ \amp = \arg\bigg(\frac{z_2-z_0}{z_1-z_0}\bigg)\\ \amp = \angle(L_1,L_2). \end{align*}
