Proof

Suppose \(T(z) = az+b\) where \(a \neq 0\text{.}\) Since the angle between curves is defined to be the angle between their tangent lines, it is sufficient to check that the angle between two lines is preserved. Suppose \(L_1\) and \(L_2\) intersect at \(z_0\text{,}\) and \(z_i\) is on \(L_i\) for \(i = 1,2\text{,}\) as in the following diagram.

Then,

\begin{equation*} \angle(L_1,L_2) = \arg\bigg(\frac{z_2-z_0}{z_1 - z_0}\bigg)\text{.} \end{equation*}

Since general linear transformations preserve lines, \(T(L_i)\) is the line through \(T(z_0)\) and \(T(z_i)\) for \(i = 1,2\) and it follows that

\begin{align*} \angle(T(L_1),T(L_2)) \amp = \arg\bigg(\frac{T(z_2)-T(z_0)}{T(z_1)-T(z_0)}\bigg)\\ \amp = \arg\bigg(\frac{az_2+b-az_0-b}{az_1+b-az_0-b}\bigg)\\ \amp = \arg\bigg(\frac{z_2-z_0}{z_1-z_0}\bigg)\\ \amp = \angle(L_1,L_2)\text{.} \end{align*}

Thus \(T\) preserves angles.
