Solution 7.2.4.1.
  1. The distance from 0 to \(x\) (where \(0 \lt x \lt s\)) as \(k \to 0^+\text{,}\) is
    \begin{equation*} \lim_{k \to 0^+} \frac{2}{\sqrt{k}}\arctan(\sqrt{k}x) = \lim_{k \to 0^+} \frac{2\frac{1}{1+kx^2}x\frac{1}{2\sqrt{k}}}{\frac{1}{2\sqrt{k}}} \end{equation*}
    by l'Hôpital's rule, and this second limit is \(2x\text{,}\) which is twice the Euclidean distance from 0 to \(x\text{.}\)
  2. The circumference of a circle with elliptic radius \(r\text{,}\) as \(k \to 0^+\text{,}\) is
    \begin{equation*} \lim_{k \to 0^+} \frac{2\pi}{\sqrt{k}}\sin(\sqrt{k}r) = \lim_{k \to 0^+} 2\pi \cdot \frac{\cos(\sqrt{k}r)\cdot r\cdot \frac{1}{2\sqrt{k}}}{\frac{1}{2\sqrt{k}}} \end{equation*}
    by l'Hôpital's rule, and this second limit is \(2\pi r\text{.}\)
  3. The area of the circle, as \(k \to 0^+\text{,}\) is
    \begin{equation*} \lim_{k \to 0^+} \frac{4\pi}{k}\sin^2 \left(\frac{r\sqrt{k}}{2}\right) \end{equation*}
    which can also be evaluated using l'Hôpital's rule to obtain \(\pi r^2\text{.}\)
