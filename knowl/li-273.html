Item 7.2.3.b.
Let \(\boldsymbol{r}(t) = x\cos(t) + x\sin(t) i\) for \(0 \leq t \leq 2\pi\) denote the circle centered at 0 with elliptic radius \(r\text{.}\) To have this (elliptic) radius, \(x\) must satisfy the equation \(r = 2s \arctan(\frac{x}{s})\text{,}\) so that \(x = s\tan(\frac{r}{2s})\text{.}\) Now,
\begin{align*} {\cal L}(\boldsymbol{r}) \amp= \int_0^{2\pi} \frac{2|\boldsymbol{r}^\prime(t)|}{1 + k|\boldsymbol{r}(t)|^2}~dt\\ \amp=\int_0^{2\pi} \frac{2\cdot x}{1 + kx^2}~dt\\ \amp= 2\pi \frac{2\cdot x}{1 + kx^2}\text{.} \end{align*}
Then express this circumference in terms of the elliptic radius \(r\text{:}\)
\begin{align*} {\cal L}(\boldsymbol{r}) \amp= \frac{4\pi s \tan(\frac{r}{2s})}{1+ks^2 \tan^2(\frac{r}{2s})}\\ \amp=\frac{4\pi s \tan(\frac{r}{2s})}{1+\tan^2(\frac{r}{2s})}\cdot \frac{\cos^2(\frac{r}{2s})}{\cos^2(\frac{r}{2s})}\\ \amp=\frac{4\pi s \sin(\frac{r}{2s})\cos(\frac{r}{2s})}{\cos^2(\frac{r}{2s})+\sin^2(\frac{r}{2s})}\\ \amp= 2\pi s\sin\left(\frac{r}{s}\right)\text{.} \end{align*}
