Item 5.3.6.b.
Consider a hyperbolic line \(L\) and a smooth curve \(\boldsymbol{r}\) in \(\mathbb{D}\text{.}\) Let \(\overline{\boldsymbol{r}}\) denote the inversion of \(\boldsymbol{r}\) about \(L\text{.}\) We want \({\cal L}(\boldsymbol{r}) = {\cal L}(\overline{\boldsymbol{r}})\text{.}\) There exists a map \(T\) in \(\cal H\) that sends \(L\) to the real axis. Since \(T\) is a Möbius transformation it preserves symmetry points, so \(T(\overline{\boldsymbol{r}})\) is reflection of \(T(\boldsymbol{r})\) across the real axis. By part (a),
\begin{equation*} {\cal L}(T(\boldsymbol{r})) = {\cal L}(T(\overline{\boldsymbol{r}})) \end{equation*}
and by Theorem 5.3.7
\begin{equation*} {\cal L}(\boldsymbol{r}) = {\cal L}(\overline{\boldsymbol{r}})\text{.} \end{equation*}
