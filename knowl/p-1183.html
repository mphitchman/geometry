Recall, the circumference of a circle in \((\mathbb{D},\cal{H})\) is \(c = 2\pi \sinh(r).\) To compute the curvature, use the power series expansion

\begin{equation*} \sinh(r)=r + \frac{r^3}{3!}+\frac{r^5}{5!}+\cdots. \end{equation*}
\begin{align*} k \amp = -3 \lim_{r \to 0^+}\frac{d^2}{dr^2}\bigg[\frac{2\pi \sinh(r)}{2\pi r}\bigg]\\ \amp = -3 \lim_{r \to 0^+}\frac{d^2}{dr^2}\bigg[1+\frac{r^2}{3!}+\frac{r^4}{5!}+\cdots\bigg]\\ \amp = -3 \lim_{r \to 0^+}\bigg[ \frac{1}{3}+\frac{12r^2}{5!}+\cdots\bigg]. \end{align*}
