We argue that any horocycle is congruent to the one through the origin with ideal point 1 (which is given by the equation \(|z-1/2| = 1/2\)). Call this horocycle \(C_1\text{.}\) Given an arbitrary horocycle, suppose \(z_0\) is on the horocycle and $u$ is its ideal point. By LemmaÂ 5.1.5 there is a map in \(\cal H\) that sends \(z_0\) to 0 and \(u\) to 1. Since the map preserves angles, clines, and the circle at infinity, the image of our horocycle is the horocycle \(C_1\text{.}\) Since any horocycle is congruent to this one, all horocycles are congruent.
