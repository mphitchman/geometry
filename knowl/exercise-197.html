Show that the Dirichlet domain at any point of the torus in Example 7.7.9 is an \(a\) by \(b\) rectangle by completing the following parts.

a. Construct an \(a\) by \(b\) rectangle to be the fundamental domain, and place eight copies of this rectangle around the fundamental domain as in Figure 7.7.10. Then plot a point \(x\) in the fundamental domain, and plot its image in each of the copies.

b. For each image \(x^\prime\) of \(x\text{,}\) construct the perpendicular bisector of the segment \(xx^\prime\text{.}\) The eight perpendicular bisectors enclose the Dirichlet domain based at \(x\text{.}\) Prove that the Dirichlet domain is also an \(a\) by \(b\) rectangle.
