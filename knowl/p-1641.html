Consider the 3-torus manifold in Figure 8.2, which has been reproduced in the following diagram. Focus on the front, upper left corner labeled 1 in the following figure. The front/back face identification will identify corner 1 with corner 2. The left/right face identification will identify corner 1 with corner 3. The top/bottom identification groups corners 1 and 4. Now, turning to corner 2, the face identifications send it to 1, 5 and 6; corner 3 is identified with 1, 6, and 7; and corner 7 is identified with 3, 4, and 8. Thus, all 8 corners come together at a single point.
