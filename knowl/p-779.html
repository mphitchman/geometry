In particular, suppose \(p\) and \(q\) are two points in \(\mathbb{D}\text{.}\) The transformation

\begin{equation*} T_p(z) = \frac{z-p}{1-\overline{p}z} \end{equation*}

is in \(\cal H\) and maps \(p\) to 0. The map

\begin{equation*} T_q(z) = \frac{z-q}{1-\overline{q}z} \end{equation*}

is also in \(\cal H\) and it sends \(q\) to 0. By the group nature of \(\cal H\text{,}\) the transformation \(T_q^{-1} \circ T_p\) is in \(\cal H\) and it sends \(p\) to \(q\text{.}\) Thus, any two points in $\mathbb{D}$ are congruent and \((\mathbb{D}, {\cal H})\) is homogeneous. }
