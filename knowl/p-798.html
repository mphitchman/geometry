FigureÂ 5.2.7 illustrates an unusual feature of parallel lines in hyperbolic geometry: there is no notion of transitivity. In Euclidean geometry we know that if line \(L\) is parallel to line \(M\text{,}\) and line \(M\) is parallel to line \(N\text{,}\) then line \(L\) is parallel to line \(N\text{.}\) This is not the case in hyperbolic geometry.
