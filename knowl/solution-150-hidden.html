An elliptic circle with elliptic radius \(r\) centered at the origin intersects the positive real axis at \(x\) where \(2\arctan(x) = r\text{,}\) or \(x = \tan(r/2)\text{.}\) So, the circle may be parameterized by

\begin{equation*} \boldsymbol{r}(t) = \tan(r/2)\cos(t) + \tan(r/2)\sin(t) i~~~\text{ for }~ 0 \leq t \leq 2\pi\text{.} \end{equation*}

Notice that \(|\boldsymbol{r}^\prime(t)| = \tan(r/2)\) and \(|\boldsymbol{r}(t)|^2 = \tan^2(r/2)\) are both independent of \(t\text{,}\) so

\begin{align*} {\cal L}(\boldsymbol{r}) \amp= \int_0^{2\pi} \frac{2\cdot\tan(r/2)}{1 + \tan^2(r/2)}~dt\\ \amp= 2\pi \cdot \left(\frac{2\cdot \tan(r/2)}{1 + \tan^2(r/2)}\right)\\ \amp= 2\pi\cdot \left(\frac{2\sin(r/2)\cos(r/2)}{\cos^2(r/2)+\sin^2(r/2)}\right)\\ \amp= 2\pi\sin(r)\text{.} \end{align*}
