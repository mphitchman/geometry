Definition 4.1.1.

A collection \(G\) of transformations of a set \(A\) is called a group of transformations if \(G\) has the following three properties:

  1. Identity: \(G\) contains the identity transformation \(T:A \to A\) defined by \(T(a) = a\) for all \(a \in A\text{.}\)

  2. Closure: If \(T\) and \(S\) are two transformations in \(G\text{,}\) then the composition \(T \circ S\) is in \(G\text{.}\)

  3. Inverses: If \(T\) is in \(G\text{,}\) then the inverse \(T^{-1}\) is in \(G\text{.}\)
