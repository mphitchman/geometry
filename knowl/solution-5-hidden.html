1. \(C = 2\pi r\) for any circle.
  2. In \(S(\theta)\) with \(\theta \lt 2\pi\text{,}\) a circle of radius \(r\) centered at the cone point will have circumference \(\theta r\) which is less than \(2\pi r\text{.}\)
  3. In \(S(\theta)\) with \(\theta > 2\pi\text{,}\) a circle of radius \(r\) centered at the saddle point will have circumference \(\theta r\) which is greater than \(2\pi r\text{.}\)
