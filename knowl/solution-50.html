Solution 3.3.3.1.

Suppose \(z = x + yi \neq \infty\text{,}\) and \(\phi^{-1}(x+yi) = (a,b,c)\text{.}\) Then we have these three equations:

\begin{align*} \frac{a}{1-c} \amp = x \tag{1} \\ \frac{b}{1-c} \amp = y \tag{2} \\ a^2 + b^2+ c^2 \amp = 1 \tag{3}\text{.} \end{align*}

From equations (1) and (2) we have

\begin{equation*} x^2+y^2 = \frac{a^2+b^2}{(1-c)^2} \end{equation*}

which may be simplified, using equation (3) to

\begin{equation*} x^2+y^2 = \frac{1-c^2}{(1-c)^2} = \frac{1+c}{1-c}\text{.} \end{equation*}

Solve for \(c\) to obtain

\begin{equation*} c = \frac{x^2+y^2-1}{x^2+y^2+1}\text{.} \end{equation*}

Then solve for \(a\) and \(b\) using equations (1) and (2) to obtain the inverse function.
