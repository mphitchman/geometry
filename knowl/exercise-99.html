Exercise 3

Suppose \(p\) and \(q\) are distinct, finite points in \(\mathbb{C}^+\text{.}\) Let \(G\) consist of all elliptic Möbius transformations  that fix \(p\) and \(q\text{.}\) We consider the geometry \((\mathbb{C}^+,G)\text{.}\)

a. Show that \(G\) is a group of transformations.

b. Determine a minimally invariant set in \((\mathbb{C}^+,G)\) that contains the Euclidean line through \(p\) and \(q\text{.}\)

c. Determine a minimally invariant set in \((\mathbb{C}^+,G)\) that contains the perpendicular bisector of segment \(pq\text{.}\)

d. For any point \(z \neq p, q\) in \(\mathbb{C}^+\text{,}\) characterize all points in \(\mathbb{C}^+\) congruent to \(z\text{.}\)

e. Is \((\mathbb{C}^+,G)\) homogeneous?
