The cline equation.

Given \(c, d \in \mathbb{R}\text{,}\) \(\alpha \in \mathbb{C}\text{,}\) if \(c \neq 0\text{,}\) the cline equation

\begin{equation*} cz\overline{z} + \alpha z + \overline{\alpha} \overline{z} + d = 0 \end{equation*}

gives a circle with center \(z_0\) and radius \(r\text{,}\) where

\begin{equation*} z_0=\bigg(-\frac{\text{Re}(\alpha)}{c}, \frac{\text{Im}(\alpha)}{c}\bigg)~~~\text{and}~~~ r = \sqrt{\frac{|\alpha|^2-cd}{c^2}}\text{,} \end{equation*}

so long as \(|\alpha|^2 > cd\text{.}\) If \(c = 0\text{,}\) the cline equation gives a line.
