The Pythagorean theorem appears as Proposition 47 at the end of Book I of Euclid's Elements, and we present Euclid's proof below. The Pythagorean theorem is fundamental to the systems of measurement we utilize in this text, in both Euclidean and non-Eucidean geometries. We also remark that the final proposition of Book I, Proposition 48, gives the converse that builders use: If we measure the legs of a triangle and find that \(c^2 = a^2 + b^2\) then the angle opposite \(c\) is right.
