The clines \(C_1\) and \(C_2\) are type I clines of \(1\) and \(-1\) and the clines \(L_1\) and \(L_2\) are type II clines of \(1\) and \(-1\text{,}\) so they meet at right angles. Opposite sides have equal length since each side is the hyperbolic reflection of the side opposite to it. Indeed, reflection across the real axis takes the sides on \(C_1\) and \(C_2\) to one another, and reflection across the imaginary axis takes the sides on \(L_1\) and \(L_2\) to one another. These reflections are hyperbolic reflections, and these reflections preserve distance.
