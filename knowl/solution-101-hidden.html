We show \(|z|^2 \lt 1\) implies \(|T(z)|^2 \lt 1\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} |T(z)|^2 \amp = T(z)\cdot\overline{T(z)} \\ \amp = \frac{(z-z_0)(\overline{z}-\overline{z_0})}{(1-\overline{z_0}z)(1-z_0\overline{z})}\\ \amp = \frac{|z|^2-z\overline{z_0}-\overline{z}z_0+|z_0|^2}{1 - z\overline{z_0}-\overline{z}z_0+ |z_0|^2|z|^2}\text{.} \end{align*}

It follows that \(|T(z)|^2 \lt 1\) if and only if

\begin{align*} |z|^2-z\overline{z_0}-\overline{z}z_0+|z_0|^2 \lt 1 - z\overline{z_0}-\overline{z}z_0+ |z_0|^2|z|^2 \amp \iff |z|^2 + |z_0|^2 \lt 1 + |z_0|^2|z|^2 \\ \amp \iff |z|^2(1-|z_0|^2) \lt 1 - |z_0|^2 \\ \amp \iff |z|^2 \lt 1\text{.} \end{align*}

Thus, \(|z|^2 \lt 1 \Rightarrow |T(z)|^2 \lt 1\text{.}\)
