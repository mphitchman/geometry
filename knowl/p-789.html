Maps in \(\cal H\) are Möbius transformations that map \(\cal D\) to itself. The identity map \(T(z) = z\) certainly satisfies both conditions, so this map is in \(\cal H\text{.}\) Second, if \(T\) and \(S\) are both in \(\cal H\text{,}\) then so is \(T \circ S\) since the composition of two Möbius transformations is a Möbius transformation, and the composition will still map \(\cal D\) to itself. Third, if \(T\)m> is in \(\cal H\text{,}\) then its inverse will be a Möbius transformation that also maps \(\cal D\) to itself.
