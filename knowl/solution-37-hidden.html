Again, we have similar triangles, \(\Delta z_0z^*T\) and \(\Delta z_0Tz\text{.}\) Note that angle \(\angle z_0Tz = \pi/2\) since \(z_0z\) is the diameter of a circle containing \(T\text{.}\) It follows that

\begin{equation*} \frac{|z_0-z^*|}{r}=\frac{r}{|z_0-z|} \end{equation*}

from which we obtain \(|z-z_0||z^*-z_0| = r^2\text{.}\)
