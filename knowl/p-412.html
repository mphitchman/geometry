The circle has center \((a,b)\) and radius \(r\text{,}\) so the circle has cline equation constants \(c = 1\text{,}\) and \(\alpha = -a + bi\text{.}\) Then \(r = \sqrt{|\alpha|^2-d}\) so \(d = a^2+b^2-r^2\text{.}\) Then, inversion in the unit circle takes all \(z\) on the given circle to \(w = 1/\overline{z}\text{,}\) and these \(w\) live on a cline too. In particular, the given circle has cline equation

\begin{equation*} z\overline{z}+\alpha z+\overline{\alpha}\overline{z} + d = 0\text{.} \end{equation*}

Dividing by \(z\overline{z}\) we have

\begin{equation*} dw\overline{w} + \alpha w + \overline{\alpha}\overline{w}+1 = 0 \end{equation*}

which is a circle provided \(d \neq 0\text{.}\) And this circle has center \((\frac{a}{d},\frac{b}{d})\) and radius

\begin{equation*} \sqrt{\frac{|\alpha|^2-d}{d^2}} = \sqrt{\frac{r^2}{d^2}}\text{.} \end{equation*}
