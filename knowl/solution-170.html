Solution 7.3.1.1.

If \(T\) is a MÃ¶bius transformation in \(G_k\) where \(k \lt 0\text{,}\) then \(T\) sends \(S_k\) to itself, and some point \(z_0\) in \(S_k\) gets sent to 0. Since the circle at infinity is mapped to itself and \(T\) must preserve symmetry points, the point symmetric to \(z_0\) with respect to the circle at infinity, \(z_0^* = \frac{-1}{k\overline{z}}\text{,}\) gets sent to \(\infty\text{.}\) Moreover, some point \(z_1\) on the circle at infinity (so \(|z_1| = \frac{1}{\sqrt{|k|}}\)) gets sent to the point \(\frac{1}{\sqrt{|k|}}\text{.}\) The transformation that moves the three points \(z_0\text{,}\) \(z_0^*\text{,}\) and \(z_1\) as advertised may be described algebraically by a scaled version of the cross ratio:

\begin{equation*} T(z) = \frac{z-z_0}{z-z_0^*}\cdot \frac{z_1 - z_0^*}{z_1 - z_0}\cdot \frac{1}{\sqrt{|k|}}\text{.} \end{equation*}

But then,

\begin{align*} T(z) \amp= \frac{z - z_0}{z + \frac{1}{k\overline{z_0}}}\cdot \frac{z_1+ \frac{1}{k\overline{z_0}}}{z_1 - z_0}\cdot \frac{1}{\sqrt{|k|}}\\ \amp= \frac{z-z_0}{k\overline{z_0}z+1}\cdot \frac{k\overline{z_0}z_1 + 1}{z_1 - z_0}\cdot\frac{1}{\sqrt{|k|}}\\ \amp= \frac{z-z_0}{k\overline{z_0}z+1}\cdot \frac{k\overline{z_0}z_1 -kz_1\overline{z_1}}{z_1 - z_0}\cdot\frac{1}{\sqrt{|k|}}\\ \amp=\frac{z-z_0}{k\overline{z_0}z+1}\cdot \frac{kz_1(\overline{z_0} -\overline{z_1})}{z_1 - z_0}\cdot\frac{1}{\sqrt{|k|}}\text{.} \end{align*}

Now, we claim

\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{\sqrt{|k|}}\cdot\frac{kz_1(\overline{z_0} -\overline{z_1})}{z_1 - z_0} = e^{i\theta} \end{equation*}

for some \(\theta\text{.}\) Indeed,

\begin{equation*} \left|\frac{1}{\sqrt{|k|}}\cdot\frac{kz_1(\overline{z_0} -\overline{z_1})}{z_1 - z_0}\right| = |z_1|\sqrt{|k|} = 1\text{,} \end{equation*}

and it follows that

\begin{equation*} T(z) = e^{i\theta}\frac{z-z_0}{1+k\overline{z_0}z}\text{.} \end{equation*}
