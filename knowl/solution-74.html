Solution 3.5.8.1.
  1. Since \(T\) is elliptic it has normal form
    \begin{equation*} \frac{T(z)-p}{T(z)-q}=e^{i\theta}\frac{z-p}{z-q}\text{,} \end{equation*}
    and plugging in \(z_\infty\) we obtain
    \begin{equation*} 1 = e^{i\theta}\frac{z_\infty-p}{z_\infty-q} \end{equation*}
    so that
    \begin{equation*} |z_\infty - q| = |z_\infty - p| \end{equation*}
    which means that \(z_\infty\) lives on the line perpendicular to segment \(pq\text{.}\) By LemmaÂ 3.5.7, \(w_\infty -p = z_\infty - q\) and \(w_\infty - q = z_\infty - p\) so \(w_\infty\) is on this perpendicular as well.
  2. Since \(z_\infty\) is on the perpendicular of segment \(pq\) there exists a circle centered at \(z_\infty\) that goes through \(p\) and \(q\text{.}\) Inversion about this circle sends \(z_\infty\) to \(\infty\) and leaves \(p\) and \(q\) fixed. This inversion also takes \(\infty\) to \(z_\infty\text{.}\) Then, reflect about the line through \(p\) and \(q\text{.}\) The composition of these two inversions fixes \(p\) and \(q\text{,}\) takes \(z_\infty\) to \(\infty\) and \(\infty\) to \(w_\infty\text{.}\)
