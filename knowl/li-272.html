Item 7.2.3.a.
Let \(\boldsymbol{r}(t) = t + 0i\) where \(0 \leq t \leq x\) describe the line segment from 0 to \(x\) where \(0 \lt x \lt s = 1/\sqrt{k}\text{.}\) Then, with a substitution of \(u = \sqrt{k}t\) below,
\begin{align*} {\cal L}(\boldsymbol{r}) \amp= \int_0^x \frac{2|\boldsymbol{r}^\prime(t)|}{1 + k|\boldsymbol{r}(t)|^2}~dt\\ \amp=\int_0^x \frac{2\cdot 1}{1 + kt^2}~dt\\ \amp= \frac{2}{\sqrt{k}}\int_0^x \frac{\sqrt{k}}{1+(\sqrt{k}t)^2}~dt\\ \amp=\frac{2}{\sqrt{k}}\int_0^{\sqrt{k}x}\frac{1}{1+u^2}~du\\ \amp= \frac{2}{\sqrt{k}}\arctan(\sqrt{k}x)\\ \amp= 2s \arctan\left(\frac{x}{s}\right)\text{.} \end{align*}
