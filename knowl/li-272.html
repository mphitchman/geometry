Item 7.2.3.c.
Suppose the circle with elliptic radius \(r\) is centered at the origin. Let \(x\) denote its Euclidean radius (and note that \(r = 2s\arctan(\frac{x}{s}))\text{.}\) The area of the circle, in terms of the Euclidean radius \(x\) is thus
\begin{align*} A \amp= \int_0^{2\pi}\int_0^x\frac{4r}{(1+kr^2)^2}~dr~d\theta\\ \amp= \int_0^{2\pi}\frac{-2}{k(1+kr^2)}\bigg|_0^x~d\theta\\ \amp= 2\pi\left(\frac{2}{k}-\frac{2}{k(1+kx^2)}\right)\\ \amp= 4\pi\frac{k^2x^2}{k^2(1+kx^2)}\\ \amp= 4\pi \frac{x^2}{1+kx^2}\text{.} \end{align*}
To express the area in terms of the elliptic radius \(r\text{,}\) set \(x = s\tan(\frac{r}{2s})\) so that
\begin{equation*} A = \frac{4\pi s^2\tan^2(\frac{r}{2s})}{1 + ks^2\tan^2(\frac{r}{2})}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Multiplying top and bottom by \(\cos^2(r/2s)\) and noting that \(ks^2 = 1\) it follows that
\begin{equation*} A = 4\pi s^2 \sin^2\left(\frac{r}{2s}\right)\text{.} \end{equation*}
