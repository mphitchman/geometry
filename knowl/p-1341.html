The parallax is \(p = .3\) arcseconds, which translates to \(1.454 \times 10^{-6}\) radians. The Earth-Sun radius is \(d = 1.579 \times 10^{-5}\) light-years. If the universe is hyperbolic, this places the following bound on the curvature:

\begin{align*} |k| \amp\lt \left[ \frac{\ln(\tan(\pi/4-p))}{2d}\right]^2 \\ \amp\approx 2.12 \times 10^{-3} ~\text{light~years}^{-2}\text{.} \end{align*}

Thus, if the universe is hyperbolic, its curvature is bounded by

\begin{equation*} -.00212 \lt k \lt 0 \end{equation*}

in units light~years\(^{-2}\text{.}\)
