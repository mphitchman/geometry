In particular, first invert the given hexagon across the cline \(C\) containing the \(a\) edge. Then rotate the image hexagon by 60 degrees about its hyperbolic center \(q\text{.}\) This center is the point symmetric to the origin with respect to the cline \(C\text{,}\) and as a hyperbolic rotation it is the composition of two hyperbolic reflections intersecting at \(q\text{.}\) The composition of an odd number of hyperbolic reflections is not a transformation in \(\cal H\text{,}\) but it is an isometry of the hyperbolic plane, an orientation reversing isometry, and such isometries may be used to determine a quotient space.
