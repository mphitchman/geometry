Suppose \(p\) and \(q\) are distinct points in \(\mathbb{P}^2\text{.}\) This means \(q \neq p_a\) as points in \(\mathbb{C}^+\text{.}\) Construct the antipodal point \(p_a\text{,}\) which gives us three distinct points in \(\mathbb{C}^+\text{:}\) \(p,q\) and \(p_a\text{.}\) There exists a unique cline through these three points.
