Proof

To compute the area of a lune, first move the vertex of the lune to the origin in such a way that one leg of the lune lies on the real axis, as in FigureÂ 6.3.10. Then half of the lunar region can be described in polar coordinates by \(0 \leq r \leq 1\) and \(0 \leq \theta \leq \alpha\text{.}\)

So the area of a lune having angle \(\alpha\) is given by

\begin{align*} A \amp = 2\int_0^\alpha\int_0^1 \frac{4r}{(1+r^2)^2}dr d\theta \tag{Let $u = 1+r^2$}\\ \amp = 2\int_0^\alpha \bigg[\frac{-2}{1+r^2} \bigg|_0^1\bigg]~d\theta\\ \amp = 2\int_0^\alpha 1 ~d\theta\\ \amp = 2\alpha. \end{align*}
