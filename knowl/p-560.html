Suppose \(T(z) = (az+b)/(cz+d)\) is a Möbius transformation that fixes 0 and \(\infty\text{.}\) In this case, the form of the Möbius transformation can be simplified. In particular, since \(T(0) = 0\text{,}\) it follows that \(b=0\text{.}\) And since \(T(\infty)=\infty\text{,}\) it follows that \(c = 0\text{.}\) Thus, \(T(z) = \frac{a}{d}z\) which may be written as

\begin{equation*} T(z) = re^{i\theta}z\text{.} \end{equation*}
