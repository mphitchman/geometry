Consider the Poincaré dodecahedral space in Example 8.1.4, which has been reproduced in the following diagram. Each corner belongs to 3 faces, so will be directly identified with 3 other corners. Then each of these 3 corners is itself identified with three corners. Convince yourself that the corners are identified in groups of 4 as indicated in the following figure. In particular, the 4 circles form a group. That is, each identification of faces containing a circle corner sends the circle corner to another circle corner. Similarly, the 4 square corners come together at a single point, as do the 4 star corners and the 4 oval corners. The 4 unlabeled corners form the 5th group of 4 corners. Thus, the 20 corners determine 5 distinct points in the manifold.
