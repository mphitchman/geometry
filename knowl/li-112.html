Item 2.

\(\cal T\) has closure: Suppose \(T_b\) and \(T_c\) are in \(\cal T\text{.}\) Then \(T_b \circ T_c(z) = T_b(z + c) = (z+c)+b = z + (b+c)\text{.}\) But this map is exactly the translation \(T_{b+c}\text{,}\) which is in \(\cal T\) since \(b + c \in \mathbb{C}\text{.}\) Thus, the composition of two translations is again a translation. Notationally, we have shown that \(T_b \circ T_c(z) = T_{b+c}(z)\text{.}\)
