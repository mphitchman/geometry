Suppose \(p\) and \(q\) are arbitrary points in \(\mathbb{C}\text{.}\) We must find a translation \(T\) in \(\cal{T}\) such that \(T(p) = q\text{.}\) Let \(w = q - p\text{,}\) and consider the translation \(T_w\) in \(\cal{T}\text{.}\) Then \(T_w(p) = p + w = p + (q - p) = q\text{.}\) Thus \(T_w(p) = q\) and \(p \cong q\text{.}\) Since \(p\) and \(q\) are arbitrary points in \(\mathbb{C}\) it follows that \((\mathbb{C},\cal{T})\) is homogeneous.
