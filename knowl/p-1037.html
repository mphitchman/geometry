A rotation of \(\mathbb{D}\) about a point \(z_0\) sends points in \(\mathbb{D}\) around type II clines of \(z_0\) and \(z_0^*\text{,}\) the point symmetric to \(z_0\) with respect to the unit circle. The map \(V\) sends \(z_0\) and \(z_0^*\) to points \(w_0\) and \(w_0^*\) that are symmetric with respect to the real axis. Since \(V\) preserves type II clines, the image of a hyperbolic rotation over in the upper half-plane is a map that pushes points around type two clines of \(w_0\) and \(w_0^*\text{,}\) suggesting a rotation of \(\mathbb{U}\) about \(w_0\) along Euclidean circles that correspond to type II clines of \(w_0\) and its reflection across the real axis.
