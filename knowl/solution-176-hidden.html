In a 2007 paper, {\it Astrometry of Galactic Star Forming Region Sharpless 269 with VERA : Parallax Measurements and Constraint on Outer Rotation Curve} \par ({\tt http://arxiv.org/abs/0709.0820v1}) the authors detect a parallax of \(189 \pm 8 \mu as = 1.89 \times 10^{-4}\) arc seconds, or \(9.16\times 10^{-10}\) radians. The curvature constraint formula places the bound

\begin{equation*} |k|\lt 8.42 \times 10^{-10} ~\text{light-years}^{-2}\text{,} \end{equation*}

so that if the universe is hyperbolic, its curvature is bounded by

\begin{equation*} -8.42 \times 10^{-10} \lt k \lt 0 \end{equation*}

in units light-years\(^{-2}\text{.}\)
