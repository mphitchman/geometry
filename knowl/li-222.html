Item 5.4.14.a.
Determine the hyperbolic distance between \(p\) and the origin (corner two of the journey). In particular, show that \(\cosh(d_H(0,p)) = \cosh^2(a)\text{.}\) Note that if this journey had been done in the Euclidean plane, the corresponding distance would be \(\sqrt{2}a\text{.}\) How do these distances compare for small positive values of \(a\text{?}\) Is \(\cosh^2(a)\) close to \(\cosh(\sqrt{2}a)\text{?}\)
