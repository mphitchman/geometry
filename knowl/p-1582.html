\(H_g\) can be modeled by a 4\(g\)-gon in \(\mathbb{D}\) whose interior angles are each \(\frac{2\pi}{4g}\text{.}\) For each pair of edges in the \(4g\)-gon there exists a transformation in \((\mathbb{D}, {\cal H})\) as in Example 7.7.9 that takes an edge onto the other. Call \(\Gamma\) the group generated by the \(2g\) transformations. Then \(H_g\) is homeomorphic to the quotient space \(\mathbb{D}/\Gamma\text{.}\)
