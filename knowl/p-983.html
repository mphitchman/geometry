Inductive Step: \(n \gt 3\text{.}\) Assuming that the area formula holds for all \(k\)-gons for \(k = 3, \cdots, n-1\text{,}\) we prove the formula holds for \(n\)-gons as well. Suppose an \(n\)-gon in the hyperbolic plane has vertices \(v_1, v_2, \cdots, v_n\) and that the interior angle at vertex \(v_i\) is \(\alpha_i\text{.}\) Call this \(n\)-gon \(P_n\text{.}\) Construct the hyperbolic segment connecting \(v_1\) and \(v_{n-1}\text{.}\) This partitions the \(n\)-gon into a triangle (\(\Delta\)) and an \((n-1)\)-gon (\(P_{n-1}\)) as pictured in the following diagram.
