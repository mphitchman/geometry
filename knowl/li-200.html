Item 5.4.9.a.
The center of the circle containing hyperbolic segment \(v_0v_1\text{,}\) denoted \(z_0\text{,}\) has real component equal to \(b\text{,}\) as indicated in FigureÂ 5.4.22. The point \(b\) is the midpoint of Euclidean segment \(v_0 v_0^*\text{,}\) where \(v_0 = r\) and so \(v_0^* = \frac{1}{r}\text{.}\) Thus
\begin{equation*} b = \frac{r + \frac{1}{r}}{2} = \frac{1+r^2}{2r}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Since \(\angle b0z_0 = \pi/8\) it follows that the imaginary component of \(z_0\) is \(b \cdot \tan(\pi/8)\text{.}\) That is,
\begin{equation*} z_0 = \frac{1+r^2}{2r} + \frac{1+r^2}{2r}\tan(\pi/8)i\text{.} \end{equation*}
