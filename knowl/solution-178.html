Solution 7.4.2.1.

We evaluate the integral

\begin{equation*} \int_0^{2\pi} \int_0^x \frac{4r}{(1+kr^2)^2}dr d\theta \end{equation*}

by considering the cases \(k=0\) and \(k \neq 0\text{.}\)

If \(k=0\) the integral becomes

\begin{equation*} \int_0^{2\pi} \int_0^x 4rdr d\theta\text{,} \end{equation*}

which evaluates to \(4\pix^2\text{,}\) and, since \(k=0\) we have the desired result.

If \(k \neq 0\) we tackle the integral by the \(u\)-substituion \(=1+kr^2\) to obtain the result.
