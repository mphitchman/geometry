The four hexagons represent a cell division of \(H_2\) having 6 vertices, 12 edges, and 4 faces. In the edge identification, corners come together in groups of 4, so we need each corner angle to equal \(90^\circ\) in order to endow it with a homogeneous geometry. We know we can do this in the hyperbolic plane. Moreover, according to TheoremÂ 5.4.19, there is freedom in choosing the dimensions of the hexagons. That is, for each triple of real numbers \((a,b,c)\) there exists a right-angled hexagon in \(\mathbb{D}\) with alternate lengths \(a,b,\) and \(c\text{.}\) So, there exists a two-holed torus for each combination of six seam lengths (\(a_1,a_2,a_3,b_1,b_2,\) and \(b_3\)).
