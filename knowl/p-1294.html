Assume \(T\) is a Möbius transformation in \(G_k\) with \(k \gt 0\text{.}\) That is, if \(z_a = \frac{-1}{k\overline{z}}\) then

\begin{align*} T(z) \amp= \frac{-1}{k\overline{T(z_a)}}\tag{1} \text{.} \end{align*}

Assume

\begin{equation*} T(z) = \frac{az+b}{cz+d} \end{equation*}

and \(ad - bc = 1\text{.}\) Then

\begin{align*} \overline{T\left(\frac{-1}{k\overline{z}}\right)} \amp= \overline{\left[\frac{a(\frac{-1}{k\overline{z}})+b}{c(\frac{-1}{k\overline{z}})+d}\right]}\\ \amp= \frac{\frac{-\overline{a}}{kz} + \overline{b}}{\frac{-\overline{c}}{kz} + \overline{d}}\\ \amp= \frac{k\overline{b}z - \overline{a}}{k\overline{d}z - \overline{c}}\text{.} \end{align*}

Plugging this expression into equation (1) above yields

\begin{align*} T(z) \amp= \frac{-1}{k\left[\frac{k\overline{b}z - \overline{a}}{k\overline{d}z - \overline{c}}\right]}\\ \amp= \frac{-k\overline{d}z + \overline{c}}{k^2\overline{b}z - k\overline{a}}\text{.} \end{align*}

One checks the determinant of this Möbius transformation is \(k^2\) since \(ad - bc = 1\) so

\begin{equation*} \frac{az+b}{cz+d} = \frac{-\overline{d}z + \frac{1}{k}\overline{c}}{k\overline{b}z-\overline{a}} \end{equation*}

are equal transformations with determinant equal to 1. Since this Möbius transformation is unique up to sign, we assume \(d = -\overline{a}\) and \(c = k\overline{b}\text{.}\) Plugging in to the original description of \(T\) we have

\begin{align*} T(z) \amp= \frac{az+b}{k\overline{b}z - \overline{a}}\\ \amp= \frac{-\frac{a}{\overline{a}}z - \frac{b}{\overline{a}}}{-k\frac{\overline{b}}{\overline{a}}z+1}\\ \amp= \frac{-\frac{a}{\overline{a}}(z + \frac{b}{a})}{1 - k\overline{[\frac{b}{a}]}z}\\ \amp= e^{i\theta}\frac{z - z_0}{1 + k\overline{z_0}z}\text{,} \end{align*}

where \(e^{i\theta} =-\frac{a}{\overline{a}}\) and \(z_0 = -\frac{b}{a}\text{.}\)
