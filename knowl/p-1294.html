The line through the north pole \(N = (0,0,\frac{1}{\sqrt{k}})\) and a point \(P = (a,b,c) \neq N\) on the sphere may be expressed parametrically as \({\cal L}(t) = \langle 0,0,\frac{1}{\sqrt{k}}\rangle + t\langle a-0, b-0, c - \frac{1}{\sqrt{k}}\rangle\text{.}\) This line intersects the \(xy\)-plane when

\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{\sqrt{k}}+t\left(c -\frac{1}{\sqrt{k}}\right) = 0\text{.} \end{equation*}

This intersection occurs when \(t = \frac{1}{1-\sqrt{k}c}\text{.}\) So,

\begin{equation*} \phi_k((a,b,c)) = \frac{a}{1-c\sqrt{k}}+\frac{b}{1-c\sqrt{k}}i\text{.} \end{equation*}
