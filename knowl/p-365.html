The Pythagorean theorem applied to \(\Delta pqo\) gives

\begin{equation*} |p-q|^2 + d^2 = s^2,\tag{1} \end{equation*}

and the Pythagorean theorem applied to \(\Delta nqo\) gives

\begin{equation*} |q-n|^2 + d^2 = r^2. \tag{2} \end{equation*}

By subtracting equation (2) from (1), we have

\begin{equation*} |p-q|^2-|q-n|^2 = s^2-r^2\text{,} \end{equation*}

which factors as

\begin{equation*} (|p-q|-|q-n|)(|p-q|+|q-n|) = s^2-r^2\text{.} \end{equation*}

Since \(|p-q|-|q-n| = |p-m|\) and \(|p-q| + |q-n| = |p-n|\text{,}\) the result follows.
