Solution 3.5.12.1.

If \(T\) is parabolic, it has normal form

\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{T(z)-p}=\frac{1}{z-p}+d \end{equation*}

for some complex constant \(d\text{.}\) To show that the line through \(p\) and \(p+1/d\) gets sent to itself, we track the image of three points on this line: \(\infty\text{,}\) \(p\text{,}\) and \(p+1/d\text{.}\)

Plug \(z = \infty\) into the normal form to obtain

\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{T(\infty)-p}=d, \end{equation*}

so that \(T(\infty) = p + \frac{1}{d}\text{.}\) Second, \(T(p) = p\text{,}\) since \(p\) is the single fixed point of the map. Finally, if \(z = p+\frac{1}{d}\text{,}\) the normal form reveals

\begin{equation*} T\bigg(p+\frac{1}{d}\bigg) = \frac{1}{2d} + p\text{.} \end{equation*}

Now \(p+\frac{1}{2d}\) is on the line through \(p\) and \(p+\frac{1}{d}\) so we've got three points on the line getting sent to three points on the line. Thus the entire line gets sent to itself.
