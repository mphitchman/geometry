The theorem holds in general because M\amp#xf6;bius transformations preserve clines and angles, and maps in \(\cal H\) are M\amp#xf6;bius transformations. Thus, if \(z_0 \neq 0\text{,}\) move \(z_0\) to the origin by a map \(T\) in \(\cal H\text{.}\) Find the two parallel lines as in our argument in the proof, then apply \(T^{-1}\) to the situation. The two lines remain hyperbolic lines parallel to \(L\text{.}\)
