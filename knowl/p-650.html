The reader who has seen group theory will know that in addition to the three properties listed in our definition, the group operation must satisfy a property called associativity. In the context of transformations, the group operation is composition of transformations, and this operation is always associative: if \(R,S\text{,}\) and \(T\) are transformations of a set \(A\text{,}\) then the transformation \((R \circ S) \circ T\) equals the transformation \(R \circ (S \circ T)\text{.}\) So, in the present context of transformations, we omit associativity as a property that needs checking.
