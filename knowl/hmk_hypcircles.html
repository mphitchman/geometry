Exercise 5.

Constructing a hyperbolic circle centered at a point \(p\) through a point \(q\).

Suppose \(p\) and \(q\) are two points in \(\mathbb{D}\text{,}\) and that \(q\) is not on the line through \(p\) and \(p^*\) - the point symmetric to \(p\) with respect to the unit circle.

  1. Find the center of the Euclidean circle through \(p\text{,}\) \(p^*\text{,}\) and \(q\text{.}\) Call the center point \(o\text{.}\)
  2. Construct the segment \(oq\text{.}\)
  3. Construct the perpendicular to \(oq\) at \(q\text{.}\) This perpendicular intersects the Euclidean line through \(p\) and \(p^*\text{.}\) Call the intersection point \(o^\prime\text{.}\)
  4. Construct the Euclidean circle centered at \(o^\prime\) through \(q\text{.}\)
  5. Prove that this circle is the hyperbolic circle through \(q\) centered at \(p\text{.}\)
