For each real number \(k\) the geometry \(\boldsymbol{(X_k,G_k)}\) has space

\begin{equation*} X_k = \begin{cases} \mathbb{D}_k \amp \text{ if \(k \lt 0\); } \\ \mathbb{C} \amp \text{ if \(k = 0\); } \\ \mathbb{P}^2_k \amp \text{ if \(k \gt 0\), } \end{cases} \end{equation*}

and group of transformations \(G_k\) consisting of all MÃ¶bius transformations of the form

\begin{equation*} T(z) = e^{i\theta}\frac{z-z_0}{1+k\overline{z_0}z}, \end{equation*}

where \(\theta \in \mathbb{R}\) and \(z_0\) is a point in \(X_k\text{.}\) Moreover, the unique line through two points \(p\) and \(q\) in \((X_k,G_k)\) is the unique cline through the points \(p, q\text{,}\) and \(-1/(k\overline{p})\text{.}\) A smooth curve \(\boldsymbol{r}:[a,b]\to X_k\) has arc-length given by

\begin{equation*} {\cal L}(\boldsymbol{r}) = \int_a^b \frac{2|\boldsymbol{r}^\prime(t)|}{1 + k|\boldsymbol{r}(t)|^2}~dt. \end{equation*}
