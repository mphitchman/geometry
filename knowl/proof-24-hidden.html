In the proof of LemmaÂ 3.5.7, we found that the constant \(\lambda\) in the normal form of \(T\) is

\begin{equation*} \lambda = \frac{z_\infty-p}{z_\infty-q}. \end{equation*}

It follows that \(T\) has the normal form

\begin{equation*} \frac{z-p}{z-q} = \bigg(\frac{z_\infty-p}{z_\infty-q}\bigg)\cdot\frac{T(z)-p}{T(z)-q}. \end{equation*}

Solve this expression for \(T(z)\) and reduce it using the fact that \(p+q=z_\infty+w_\infty\) to get the expression for \(T\) that appears in the statement of the theorem. The details are left to the reader.
