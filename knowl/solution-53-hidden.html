If \(T(z) = (az+b)/(cz+d)\) and \(S(z) = (ez+f)/(gz+h)\) then

\begin{align*} T\circ S(z) \amp = \frac{a\left[\frac{ez+f}{gz+h}\right]+b}{c\left[\frac{ez+f}{gz+h}\right]+d}\\ \amp= \frac{a(ez+f)+b(gz+h)}{c(ez+f)+d(gz+h)}\\ \amp= \frac{(ae+bg)z+(af+bh)}{(ce+dg)z+(cf+dh)} \end{align*}

The determinant of the map is

\begin{align*} (T\circ S)\amp= (ae+bg)(cf+dh)-(af+bh)(ce+dg)\\ \amp= bc(fg-eh)+ad(eh-fg)\\ \amp= (ad-bc)(eh-fg) \\ \amp= \text{Det}(T)\cdot \text{Det}(S)\text{.} \end{align*}

Since \(T\) and \(S\) are Möbius transformations, this product is not zero, so \(T\circ S\) is also a Möbius transformation.
