The arrival of non-Euclidean geometry soon caused a stir in circles outside the mathematics community. Fyodor Dostoevsky thought non-Euclidean geometry was interesting enough to include in The Brothers Karamazov, first published in 1880. Early in the novel two of the brothers, Ivan and Alyosha, get reacquainted at a tavern. Ivan discourages his younger brother from thinking about whether God exists, arguing that if one cannot fathom non-Euclidean geometry, then one has no hope of understanding questions about God. 2
