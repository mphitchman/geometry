Suppose \(T(z) = az + b\text{,}\) where \(a \neq 0\text{,}\) and consider the circle \(C\) given by \(|z - z_0| = r\text{.}\) The image of \(C\) consists of all points \(w\) where \(w = az + b\) and \(z\) satisfies the circle equation. Notice that

\begin{align*} r \amp = |z - z_0| \\ \amp = \frac{1}{|a|}|az - az_0| \\ \amp = \frac{1}{|a|}|(az+b)-(az_0+b)| \\ \amp = \frac{1}{|a|}|w - w_0| \end{align*}

where \(w_0 = az_0 + b\text{.}\) It follows that the image points \(w\) of the circle \(C\) satisfy the circle equation

\begin{equation*} |w - w_0| = |a|r\text{.} \end{equation*}

Thus, \(T(C)\) is a circle.
