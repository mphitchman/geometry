Such a circle does indeed exist, and we will construct it now. As in FigureÂ 5.1.4, draw circle \(C_1\) with diameter \(0z_0^*.\) Let \(p\) be a point of intersection of \(C_1\) and the unit circle \(\mathbb{S}^1_\infty\text{.}\) Construct the circle \(C\) through \(p\) centered at \(z_0^*\text{.}\) Since \(\angle 0pz_0^*\) is right, \(\mathbb{S}^1_\infty\) is orthogonal to \(C\text{,}\) so inversion about \(C\) sends \(\mathbb{S}^1_\infty\) to itself.
