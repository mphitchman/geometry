Theorem 5.4.12

Suppose a hyperbolic triangle in \(\mathbb{D}\) has angles \(\alpha\text{,}\) \(\beta\) and \(\gamma\) and opposite hyperbolic side lengths \(a\text{,}\) \(b\text{,}\) \(c\text{,}\) as pictured in FigureÂ 5.4.11. Then the following laws hold.

a. First hyperbolic law of cosines.

\begin{equation*} \cosh(c) = \cosh(a)\cosh(b)-\sinh(a)\sinh(b)\cos(\gamma). \end{equation*}

b. Second hyperbolic law of cosines.

\begin{equation*} \cosh(c) = \frac{\cos(\alpha)\cos(\beta)+\cos(\gamma)}{\sin(\alpha)\sin(\beta)}. \end{equation*}

c. Hyperbolic law of sines.

\begin{equation*} \frac{\sinh(a)}{\sin(\alpha)} = \frac{\sinh(b)}{\sin(\beta)}=\frac{\sinh(c)}{\sin(\gamma)}. \end{equation*}
