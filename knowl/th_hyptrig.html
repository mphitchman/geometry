Theorem 5.4.12.

Suppose a hyperbolic triangle in \(\mathbb{D}\) has angles \(\alpha\text{,}\) \(\beta\text{,}\) and \(\gamma\) and opposite hyperbolic side lengths \(a\text{,}\) \(b\text{,}\) \(c\text{,}\) as pictured in FigureÂ 5.4.11. Then the following laws hold.

  1. First hyperbolic law of cosines.
    \begin{equation*} \cosh(c) = \cosh(a)\cosh(b)-\sinh(a)\sinh(b)\cos(\gamma). \end{equation*}
  2. Second hyperbolic law of cosines.
    \begin{equation*} \cosh(c) = \frac{\cos(\alpha)\cos(\beta)+\cos(\gamma)}{\sin(\alpha)\sin(\beta)}\text{.} \end{equation*}
  3. Hyperbolic law of sines.
    \begin{equation*} \frac{\sinh(a)}{\sin(\alpha)} = \frac{\sinh(b)}{\sin(\beta)}=\frac{\sinh(c)}{\sin(\gamma)}\text{.} \end{equation*}
