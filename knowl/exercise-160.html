Exercise10

An intrepid tax collector lives in a country in the elliptic space \(\mathbb{P}^2\text{.}\) For collection purposes, the country is divided into triangular grids. The collector observes that the angles of the triangle she collects in are \(92^\circ\text{,}\) \(62^\circ\text{,}\) and \(27^\circ\text{.}\) What is the area of her triangle? Can the entire space \(\mathbb{P}^2\) be subdivided into a finite number of triangles? (Be sure to convert to radians.)
