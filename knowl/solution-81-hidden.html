Here's one approach. There exists a Möbius transformation \(T\) that sends \(p \mapsto 0\text{,}\) \(q \mapsto \infty\text{,}\) and \(z \mapsto 1\text{.}\) The unit circle \(\mathbb{S}^1\) is a type II cline of 0 and \(\infty\) that goes through 1. Since Möbius transformations preserve type II clines, \(T^{-1}(\mathbb{S}^1)\) is a type II cline of \(p\) and \(q\) that goes through \(z\text{.}\)
