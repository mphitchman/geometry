Solution 8.1.6.1.

If we identify opposite faces of a cube with a 1/4 turn clockwise twist, the corners come together in two groups of 4. Consider the front top left corner labeled 1 in the following diagram. The front/back face identification takes corner 1 to corner 2. The top/bottom face identification takes corner 1 to corner 4. The left/right face identification takes corner 1 to corner 3. Turning to corner 2: front/back takes 2 to 1; left/right takes 2 to 4; top/bottom takes 2 to 3. Similarly, one checks that corners 3 and 4 are identified with other corners in the group \(\{1,2,3,4\}\) of corners, so that these 4 corners come together at a single point in the manifold. The other 4 corners also come together at a single point. In Euclidean space, 4 corners can't span an entire patch of 3-dimensional space, so this isn't a Euclidean manifold.
