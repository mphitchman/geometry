A hyperbolic translation that fixes ideal points \(p\) and \(q\) in the disk model gets transferred by \(V\) to a map of the upper half-plane that moves points along type I clines of \(V(p)\) and \(V(q)\text{.}\) If neither \(p\) nor \(q\) is the point \(i\text{,}\) the translation over in \(\mathbb{U}\) moves points along circles through \(V(p)\) and \(V(q)\text{.}\) Either all points head toward \(V(p)\) and away from \(V(q)\) or vice versa, depending on the translation factor. If \(p = i\) then the translation over in \(\mathbb{U}\) moves all points along Euclidean rays emanating from \(V(q)\) as suggested in the following figure. Either every point moves away from \(V(q)\) and toward \(\infty\text{,}\) or vice versa.
