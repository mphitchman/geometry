If four distinct points live on the same cline then there is some Möbius transformation taking them all to the real axis. The cross ratio of their image points is then a real number. The cross ratio of the original 4 points is then real, by the invariance of cross ratio.

For the converse, suppose \((z_0,z_1;z_2,z_3)\) is real. We know there exists some Möbius transformation sending \(z_1 \mapsto 1\text{,}\) \(z_2 \mapsto 0\text{,}\) and \(z_3 \mapsto \infty\text{,}\) call it \(T\text{,}\) and

\begin{equation*} T(z) = \frac{z-z_2}{z-z_3}\frac{z_1-z_3}{z_1-z_2}\text{.} \end{equation*}

Note \(T(z_0)\) is a real number. So \(T(z_0)\) and \(T(z_1) = 1\) and \(T(z_2)=0\) and \(T(z_3) = \infty\) all live on the same cline (the real axis). The inverse Möbius transformation \(T^{-1}\) preserves clines, so it sends the real axis to a cline containing the four points \(z_0,z_1,z_2,z_3\text{.}\)
