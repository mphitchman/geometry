On a sphere, geodesics follow great circles. A great circle is a circle drawn on the surface of the sphere whose center (in three-dimensional space) corresponds to the center of the sphere. Put another way, a great circle is a circle of maximum diameter drawn on the sphere. The circles \(a\) and \(b\) in FigureÂ 1.3.2 are great circles, but circle \(c\) is not.
