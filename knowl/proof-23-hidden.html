Suppose \(T\) satisfies the conditions of the lemma. Then \(T\) has normal form

\begin{equation*} \frac{z-p}{z-q} = \lambda\frac{T(z)-p}{T(z)-q}\text{,} \end{equation*}

where \(\lambda = re^{i\theta}\text{.}\) Plug \(z = z_\infty\) into the normal form to see

\begin{equation*} \frac{z_\infty-p}{z_\infty-q} = \lambda\cdot 1\text{.} \end{equation*}

Plug \(z = \infty\) into the normal form to see

\begin{equation*} 1 = \lambda\frac{w_\infty-p}{w_\infty-q}\text{.} \end{equation*}

Next solve each equation for \(\lambda\text{,}\) set them equal, cross multiply, and simplify as follows to get the result:

\begin{align*} \frac{z_\infty-p}{z_\infty-q} \amp = \frac{w_\infty-q}{w_\infty-p}\\ (z_\infty-p)(w_\infty-p) \amp = (w_\infty-q)(z_\infty-q)\\ p^2-pw_\infty-pz_\infty \amp = q^2-qw_\infty-qz_\infty\\ p^2-q^2 \amp = p(z_\infty + w_\infty) - q(z_\infty + w_\infty)\\ (p-q)(p+q) \amp = (p-q)(z_\infty + w_\infty)\\ p+q \amp = z_\infty + w_\infty \amp (\text{since } p\neq q)\text{.} \end{align*}

This completes the proof.
