Item 7.3.3.b.
The circumference of a circle, as \(k \to 0^-\text{,}\) is
\begin{equation*} \lim_{s \to \infty} 2\pi s \sinh\bigg(\frac{r}{s}\bigg) = 2\pi \lim_{s \to \infty} \frac{\sinh\left(\frac{r}{s}\right)}{s^{-1}}\text{.} \end{equation*}
By l'HÃ´pital's rule, the limit is equivalent to
\begin{equation*} 2\pi \lim_{s \to \infty} \frac{\cosh\left(\frac{r}{s}\right)\frac{-r}{s^2}}{-s^{-2}} = 2\pi \lim_{s \to \infty} \cosh\left(\frac{r}{s}\right) r\text{.} \end{equation*}
Since \(\cosh(0) = 1\text{,}\) the limit is \(2\pi r\text{.}\)
