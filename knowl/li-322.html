Item 7.7.1.b.
Suppose \(z = m + ni\) is an arbitrary point in the fundamental domain (so \(0 \leq m \leq a\) and \(0 \leq n \leq b\)). The horizontally displaced images of \(z\) are \(z_a = (m + a) + ni\) and \(z_{-a} = (m - a)+ni\text{.}\) The perpendicular bisector of segment \(zz_a\) is the vertical line \(L_a\) given by \(x = m + a/2\text{.}\) The perpendicular bisector of segment \(zz_{-a}\) is the vertical line \(L_{-a}\) given by \(x = m - a/2\text{.}\) Similarly, \(z_b = m + (n+b)i\) and \(z_{-b} = m + (n-b)i\) are the vertically displaced images, and they determine the horizontal perpendicular bisectors \(L_b\) and \(L_{-b}\) given by \(y = n+b/2\) and \(y = n - b/2\text{,}\) repsectively. These four perpendicular bisectors determine an \(a \times b\) rectangle, call it \(R\text{.}\) Each diagonally displaced image of \(z\) (there are four of them in the figure above) determines a perpendicular bisector that intersects a corner of rectangle \(R\text{,}\) but does not intersect the interior of \(R\text{.}\) Thus, the rectangle \(R\) corresponds to the Dirichlet domain based at \((m,n)\text{.}\)
