Consider the hyperbolic triangle in the following figure TODO, where \(p = r\) (with \(0 \lt r \lt s = 1/\sqrt{|k|}\)), and \(q = me^{i\gamma}\) (with \(0 \lt m \lt s\)). The length of segment \(0r\) is

\begin{equation*} a = d_{H_k}(0,r) = s\ln\left(\frac{s+r}{s-r}\right)\text{,} \end{equation*}

and since \(\sqrt{|k|}s = 1\) we use Exercise 5.4.11 to see

\begin{align*} \sinh(\sqrt{|k|}a) \amp= \sinh\left(\ln\left(\frac{s+r}{s-r}\right)\right)\\ \amp= \frac{(s+r)^2-(s-r)^2}{2(s+r)(s-r)}\\ \amp= \frac{2sr}{s^2-r^2}\text{.} \end{align*}

Similarly,

\begin{align*} \cosh(\sqrt{|k|}a) \amp= \cosh\bigg(\ln\bigg(\frac{s+r}{s-r}\bigg)\bigg)\\ \amp= \frac{(s+r)^2+(s-r)^2}{2(s+r)(s-r)}\\ \amp= \frac{s^2 + r^2}{s^2-r^2}\text{.} \end{align*}

Turning to the other side,

\begin{equation*} b = d_{H_k}(0,me^{i\gamma}) = s\ln\bigg(\frac{s+m}{s-m}\bigg) \end{equation*}

so, following the derivation above,

\begin{equation*} \sinh(\sqrt{|k|}b) = \frac{2sm}{s^2-m^2}~~ \text{ and }~~\cosh(\sqrt{|k|}b) = \frac{s^2+m^2}{s^2-m^2}\text{.} \end{equation*}
