Suppose \(z = re^{i\theta}\) and \(w = se^{i\alpha}\text{.}\) Recall that for any angle \(\theta\text{,}\) \(\left|e^{i\theta}\right| = 1\text{.}\) It follows that

\begin{align*} \left|\frac{z}{w}\right| \amp = \left|\frac{r}{s}e^{i(\theta-\alpha)}\right|\\ \amp = \left|\frac{r}{s}\right| \\ \amp = \frac{|r|}{|s|} \\ \amp = \frac{|z|}{|w|} \text{.} \end{align*}

Applying the result of ExerciseÂ 2.2.4, we have

\begin{align*} \overline{(z/w)} \amp = \overline{(r/s)e^{i(\theta-\alpha)}}\\ \amp = (r/s)e^{-i(\theta-\alpha)} \\ \amp = \frac{r}{s}e^{(-i\theta)-(-i\alpha)}\\ \amp = \frac{re^{-i\theta}}{se^{-i\alpha}} \\ \amp = \frac{\overline{z}}{\overline{w}}. \text{.} \end{align*}
