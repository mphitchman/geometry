Until the late 1990s all estimates of the mass-energy content of the universe put the value of \(\Omega\) much less than 1, suggesting a hyperbolic universe. In fact, different observational techniques for estimating the total mass-energy content of the universe put the value of \(\Omega\) at about 1/3, contrary to the value of 1 predicted by the inflationary universe model.
