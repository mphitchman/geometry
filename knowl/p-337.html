Inversion in a circle centered at \(z_0\) is a transformation on the set \(\mathbb{C}-\{z_0\}\) consisting of all complex numbers except \(z_0\text{.}\) We usually denote inversion in the circle \(C\) by by \(i_C(z) = z^*\text{.}\) In the next section we will discuss how to extend this transformation in a way to include the center \(z_0\text{.}\)
