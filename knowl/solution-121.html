Solution 5.4.3.1.
  1. Use l'Hôpital's rule twice on a ratio with indeterminate form \(\frac{0}{0}\text{.}\)
    \begin{align*} \lim_{r \to 0} \frac{4\pi\sinh^2(r/2)}{\pi r^2} \amp= \lim_{r \to 0} \frac{8\sinh(\frac{r}{2})\cosh(\frac{r}{2})\frac{1}{2}}{2r} \\ \amp= \lim_{r \to 0} \frac{4[\cosh^2(\frac{r}{2})\frac{1}{2}+\sinh^2(\frac{r}{2})\frac{1}{2}]}{2}\\ \amp= \lim_{r \to 0} \left[\cosh^2\left(\frac{r}{2}\right)+\sinh^2\left(\frac{r}{2}\right)\right]\\ \amp= 1\text{,} \end{align*}
    since \(\cosh(0) = 1\) and \(\sinh(0)=0\text{.}\)

  2. Again, use l'Hôpital's rule.

    \begin{align*} \lim_{r \to 0}\frac{2\pi \sinh(r)}{2\pi r} \amp= \lim_{r \to 0}\frac{\cosh(r)}{1} \\ \amp= 1 \text{,} \end{align*}

    since \(\cosh(0) = 1\text{.}\)
