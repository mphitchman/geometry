Item 5.5.5.a.
It is remarkably convenient to transfer the situation to the upper half-plane. By a rotation in \(\mathbb{D}\) assume the ideal point common to both horocycles is \(i\text{.}\) Then \(V\) takes the horocycles to parallel, horizontal Euclidean lines in \(\mathbb{U}\) and the other legs to vertical Euclidean lines, so that the region is a Euclidean rectangle. We assume that the rectangle is determined by \(a \leq x \leq b\) and \(m \leq y \leq n\) as pictured.
