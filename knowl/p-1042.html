A \(\frac{2}{3}\)-ideal triangle in the upper half-plane with ideal vertices 1 and \(\infty\) and interior vertex on the upper half of the unit circle corresponds by \(V\) to a \(\frac{2}{3}\)-ideal triangle in the disk model with ideal points \(1\text{,}\) \(i\text{,}\) and interior vertex on the real axis. So, we argue that any \(\frac{2}{3}\)-ideal triangle in \(\mathbb{D}\) is congruent to one of this form.
