The pair separation histogram looks to have one spike of statistical significance, occurring at length dimension about 14. We can conclude this from the spike: Either we live in a torus and 14 is the smaller dimension and we can't detect the other dimension (suggesting its area is greater than \(14 \cdot 20\) square units); or we live in a Klein bottle and 14 is the orientable dimension. The non-orientable dimension can not be detected by the analysis, and could be less than or greater than 14 units. So there is no bound on the area. (Studying the point sources themselves, there is no evidence of a Klein bottle whose horizontal dimension is non-orientable.)
