Item 6.2.1.c.
Suppose \(z\) and \(z_a\) are antipodal points and
\begin{equation*} T(z) = e^{i\theta}\frac{1}{z}\text{.} \end{equation*}
We show \(T(z)\) and \(T(z_a)\) are antipodal.
\begin{align*} T(z)\overline{T(z_a)} \amp= e^{i\theta}\frac{1}{z}\cdot e^{-i\theta}\frac{1}{\overline{z_a}}\\ \amp= \frac{1}{z\cdot\overline{z_a}}\\ -1\text{,} \end{align*}
since \(z\cdot\overline{z_a}=-1\text{.}\)
