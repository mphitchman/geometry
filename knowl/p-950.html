The hyperbolic line through \(ri\) and \(si\) is the positive imaginary axis, having ideal points \(0\) and \(\infty\text{.}\) Thus,

\begin{align*} d_U(ri, si) \amp = \ln((ri, si; 0, \infty))\\ \amp = \frac{ri - 0}{ri - \infty}\cdot\frac{si-\infty}{si-0}\\ \amp = \ln\bigg(\frac{r}{s}\bigg). \end{align*}
