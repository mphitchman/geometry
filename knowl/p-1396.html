If \(k \lt 0\text{,}\)

\begin{align*} k \amp= -3 \lim_{r \to 0^+}\frac{d^2}{dr^2}\left[\frac{2\pi s \sinh(r/s)}{2\pi r}\right]\\ \amp= -3 \lim_{r \to 0^+}\frac{d^2}{dr^2}\left[\frac{\sinh(\sqrt{|k|}r)}{\sqrt{|k|}r}\right]\text{.} \end{align*}

The power series expansion for the ratio in the expression above is

\begin{equation*} \frac{\sqrt{|k|}r + (\sqrt{|k|}r)^3/3! + (\sqrt{|k|}r)^5/5!+\cdots}{\sqrt{|k|}r} = 1 + \frac{(\sqrt{|k|}r)^2}{3!} + \frac{(\sqrt{|k|}r)^4}{5!}+\cdots \end{equation*}

and its second derivative is

\begin{equation*} \frac{|k|}{3}+\frac{k^2r^2}{5\cdot 2}+\cdots~\text{.} \end{equation*}

Since each term after the first is a multiple of a positive power of \(r\text{,}\) these terms all vanish in the limit and the curvature of the space in \((S_k, G_k)\) for \(k \lt 0\) is

\begin{equation*} -3 \cdot \frac{|k|}{3} = k\text{.} \end{equation*}
