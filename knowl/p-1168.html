The curvature of the curve at a point is a measure of how drastically the curve bends away from its tangent line, and this curvature is often studied in a multivariable calculus course. The radius of curvature at a point corresponds to the radius of the circle that best approximates the curve at this point. The radius \(r\) of this circle is the reciprocal of the curvature \(k\) of the curve at the point: \(k = 1/r\text{.}\)
