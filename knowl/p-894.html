The point \(q\) can be found analytically as the point of intersection of circles \(C\) and \(C_2\text{.}\) Working it out, one finds \(q = \frac{1}{5}+\frac{2}{5}i.\) Thus,

\begin{align*} d_H(0,q)\amp =\ln\bigg(\frac{1+|q|}{1-|q|}\bigg)\\ \amp =\ln\bigg(\frac{1+\frac{1}{\sqrt{5}}}{1-\frac{1}{\sqrt{5}}}\bigg)\\ \amp =\ln\bigg(\frac{\sqrt{5}+1}{\sqrt{5}-1}\bigg)\\ \amp =\ln\bigg(\frac{(\sqrt{5}+1)^2}{(\sqrt{5}-1)(\sqrt{5}+1)}\bigg)\\ \amp =\ln\bigg(\frac{(\sqrt{5}+1)^2}{4}\bigg)\\ \amp =\ln(\varphi^2)\\ \amp =2\ln(\varphi). \end{align*}
