Solution 3.2.5.1.

Line \(L\) is given by \(y = mx + b\) with \(b \neq 0\text{.}\) From ExerciseÂ 2.4.1, \(L\) has complex line equation

\begin{equation*} (m+i)z + (m-i)\overline{z}+2b = 0. \end{equation*}

The images of all such \(z\) have the form \(w = 1/\overline{z}\text{,}\) and dividing the above line equation by \(z\overline{z}\) yields

\begin{equation*} (m+i)\frac{1}{\overline{z}}+(m-i)\frac{1}{z} + \frac{2b}{z\overline{z}} = 0\text{.} \end{equation*}

Or,

\begin{equation*} 2bw\overline{w} + (m+i)w + (m-i)\overline{w} + 0 = 0 \end{equation*}

which is the cline equation for the circle with center \((-m/2b,1/2b)\) and radius \(\sqrt{(m^2+1)/4b^2}\text{.}\)
