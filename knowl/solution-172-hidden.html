1. Letting \(k \to 0^-\) is equivalent to letting \(s \to \infty\) where \(s = 1/\sqrt{|k|}\text{.}\) By Exercise 7.3.3 the distance from 0 to \(x\) (where \(0 \lt x \lt s\)) as \(s \to \infty\text{,}\) is
    \begin{equation*} \lim_{s \to \infty} s\ln\left(\frac{s + x}{s-x}\right) = \lim_{s \to \infty} \frac{\ln\left(\frac{s + x}{s-x}\right)}{s^{-1}} \end{equation*}
    which has indeterminate form \(\frac{0}{0}\) as \(s \to \infty\text{.}\) By l'Hôpital's rule (differentiating with respect to \(s\)), the limit is equal to
    \begin{align*} \lim_{s \to \infty} \frac{\frac{1}{x+s}-\frac{1}{x-s}}{-s^{-2}}\amp= \lim_{s \to \infty} \frac{s^2}{s-x}-\frac{s^2}{s+x}\\ \amp= \lim_{s \to \infty}\frac{2s^2x}{s^2 - x^2}\\ \amp= \lim_{s \to \infty}\frac{2x}{1-\frac{x^2}{s^2}}\text{.} \end{align*}
    This last limit is \(2x\text{,}\) which is twice the Euclidean distance from 0 to \(x\text{.}\)
  2. The circumference of a circle, as \(k \to 0^-\text{,}\) is
    \begin{equation*} \lim_{s \to \infty} 2\pi s \sinh\bigg(\frac{r}{s}\bigg) = 2\pi \lim_{s \to \infty} \frac{\sinh\left(\frac{r}{s}\right)}{s^{-1}}\text{.} \end{equation*}
    By l'Hôpital's rule, the limit is equivalent to
    \begin{equation*} 2\pi \lim_{s \to \infty} \frac{\cosh\left(\frac{r}{s}\right)\frac{-r}{s^2}}{-s^{-2}} = 2\pi \lim_{s \to \infty} \cosh\left(\frac{r}{s}\right) r\text{.} \end{equation*}
    Since \(\cosh(0) = 1\text{,}\) the limit is \(2\pi r\text{.}\)
  3. The area of the circle, as \(k \to 0^+\text{,}\) is found with the help of l'Hôpital's rule:
    \begin{align*} \lim_{s \to \infty} 4\pi s^2 \sinh^2\left(\frac{r}{2s}\right) \amp= 4\pi \lim_{s \to \infty}\frac{\sinh^2(\frac{r}{2s})}{s^{-2}}\\ \amp= 4\pi \lim_{s \to \infty} \frac{ 2\sinh(\frac{r}{2s})\cosh(\frac{r}{2s})[-\frac{r}{2}s^{-2}]}{-2s^{-3}}\\ \amp= 2\pi r \lim_{s \to \infty} \frac{\sinh(\frac{r}{2s})\cosh(\frac{r}{2s})}{s^{-1}}\\ \amp= 2\pi r \lim_{s \to \infty} \frac{\cosh^2(\frac{r}{2s})[-\frac{r}{2}s^{-2}] + \sinh^2(\frac{r}{2s})[-\frac{r}{2}s^{-2}]}{-s^{-2}}\\ \amp= \pi r^2 \lim_{s \to \infty} \left[\cosh^2\left(\frac{r}{2s}\right) +\sinh^2\left(\frac{r}{2s}\right)\right] \\ \amp= \pi r^2 \left[ 1 + 0\right] \\ \amp = \pi r^2\text{.} \end{align*}
    Thus as \(k \to 0^{-}\text{,}\) the area formula for a circle approaches the Euclidean formula \(\pi r^2\text{.}\)
