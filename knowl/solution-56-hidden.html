Let \(R(z) = (z,2;1,4)\) and \(S(w) = (w,0;3,4)\text{.}\) We want \(T = S^{-1} \circ R\) so we solve the following equation for \(w\text{:}\)

\begin{equation*} \frac{z-1}{z-4}\cdot\frac{2-4}{2-1} = \frac{w-3}{w-4}\cdot \frac{0-4}{0-3}\text{.} \end{equation*}

The result is

\begin{equation*} w = \frac{-18z+36}{-5z+11} = T(z)\text{.} \end{equation*}

\(T(0) = 36/11\) and \(T(\infty)=18/5\text{.}\)
