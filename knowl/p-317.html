\(T(z) = e^{i\frac{\pi}{6}}(z-2)+2\) and \(S(z) = e^{i\frac{\pi}{4}}(z-4)+4\) and

\begin{equation*} T\circ S(z) = e^{i\frac{5\pi}{12}}(z-4) + [2e^{i\frac{\pi}{6}}+2]\text{.} \end{equation*}

We have a general linear transformation that first translates horizontally 4 units, then rotates about the origin by \(5\pi/12\text{,}\) then translates by \(2e^{i\frac{\pi}{6}}+2\text{.}\)
