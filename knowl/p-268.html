Let \(w\) denote an arbitrary element of \(\mathbb{C}\text{.}\) We must find a complex number \(z\) such that \(T(z) = w\text{.}\) To find this \(z\text{,}\) we solve \(w = az + b\) for \(z\text{.}\) So, \(z = \frac{1}{a}(w - b)\) should work (since \(a \neq 0\text{,}\) \(z\) is a complex number). Indeed, \(T(\frac{1}{a}(w - b)) = a \cdot \big[\frac{1}{a}(w - b)\big] + b = w\text{.}\) Thus, \(T\) is onto.
