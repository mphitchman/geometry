Exercise 3.

Suppose \(p\) and \(q\) are distinct, finite points in \(\mathbb{C}^+\text{.}\) Let \(G\) consist of all elliptic Möbius transformations  that fix \(p\) and \(q\text{.}\) We consider the geometry \((\mathbb{C}^+,G)\text{.}\)

  1. Show that \(G\) is a group of transformations.
  2. Determine a minimally invariant set in \((\mathbb{C}^+,G)\) that contains the Euclidean line through \(p\) and \(q\text{.}\)
  3. Determine a minimally invariant set in \((\mathbb{C}^+,G)\) that contains the perpendicular bisector of segment \(pq\text{.}\)
  4. For any point \(z \neq p, q\) in \(\mathbb{C}^+\text{,}\) characterize all points in \(\mathbb{C}^+\) congruent to \(z\text{.}\)
  5. Is \((\mathbb{C}^+,G)\) homogeneous?
