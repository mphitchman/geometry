If there were such hyperbolic lines, tangent at a point \(z_0\) in \(\mathbb{D}\text{,}\) the M\amp#xf6;bius transformation \(T(z) = 1/(z-z_0)\) maps these distinct clines to distinct, parallel Euclidean lines in \(\mathbb{C}^+\text{,}\) say \(L_1\) and \(L_2\text{.}\) Meanwhile, \(\mathbb{S}^1_\infty\) gets mapped to another circle, say \(C\text{.}\) But \(\mathbb{S}^1_\infty\) met the two hyperbolic lines at right angles, so \(C\) must must meet \(L_1\) and \(L_2\) at right angles. This means the center of \(C\) must be on \(L_1\) and \(L_2\text{,}\) which implies the two lines intersect at a point in \(\mathbb{C}\text{,}\) a contradiction.
