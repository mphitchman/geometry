Solution 3.2.10.1.

Suppose \(w\) is a point in the interior of \(D\) and that \(C\) is centered at \(z_0\text{.}\) Then ray \(z_0w\) intersects \(D\) in two points, call them \(z\) and \(z^*\text{,}\) where \(z\) is in the interior of \(C\text{.}\) Since \(D\) is orthogonal to \(C\text{,}\) \(z\) and \(z^*\) are symmetric with respect to \(C\text{.}\) Note that

\begin{equation*} |z-z_0| \lt |w-z_0| \end{equation*}

so that

\begin{align*} r^2 \amp = |z-z_0||z^*-z_0|\\ \amp \lt |w-z_0||z^*-z_0| \amp \text{(1)} \end{align*}

If we let \(w^*\) denote the point symmetric to \(w\) with respect to \(C\text{,}\) then \(|w-z_0||w^*-z_0| = r^2\text{,}\) so by comparison with (1), and noting that \(w^*\) also lies on the ray \(z_0w\text{,}\) we deduce that \(|z^*-z_0| \gt |w^*-z_0|\text{.}\)

Similarly, starting with the fact that \(|w-z_0| \lt |z^*- z_0|\) one can argue that \(|w^* - z_0| \gt |z-z_0|\text{.}\) Thus \(w^*\) lives on ray \(z_0w\) between \(z\) and \(z^*\text{,}\) and hence lives in \(D\text{.}\) That is, the interior of \(D\) is sent to itself.
