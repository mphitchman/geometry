The inversion transformations are \(i_C(z) = \frac{r^2}{\overline{z-z_0}}+z_0\) and \(i_{C^\prime}(z) = \frac{(r^\prime)^2}{\overline{z-z_0}}+z_0\text{.}\) If \(S(z) = k(z-z_0)+z_0\) is a dilation about \(z_0\) then

\begin{align*} S\circ i_{C^\prime}(z) \amp = k\left[\frac{(r^\prime)^2}{\overline{z-z_0}}+z_0-z_0\right]+z_0\\ \amp = \frac{k(r^\prime)^2}{\overline{z-z_0}}+z_0\text{.} \end{align*}

We want \(k(r^\prime)^2 = r^2\text{,}\) so our stretch factor is

\begin{equation*} k =\frac{r^2}{(r^\prime)^2}\text{.} \end{equation*}
