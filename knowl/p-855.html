Recall that in calculus we first approximate the Euclidean length of a given smooth curve \(\boldsymbol{r}(t) = x(t) + iy(t)\) by summing the contributions of small straight line segments having Euclidean length

\begin{align*} \Delta s \amp = |\boldsymbol{r}(t+\Delta t) - \boldsymbol{r}(t)|\\ \amp = \sqrt{[x(t + \Delta t) - x(t)]^2 + [y(t + \Delta t) - y(t)]^2}\\ \amp = \sqrt{\bigg[ \frac{x(t + \Delta t) - x(t)}{\Delta t}\bigg]^2 + \bigg[ \frac{y(t + \Delta t) - y(t)}{\Delta t}\bigg]^2} |\Delta t|\text{.} \end{align*}
