Solution 6.3.6.1.

Suppose the circle with elliptic radius \(r\) is centered at the origin. Let \(x\) denote its Euclidean radius (and note that \(r = 2\arctan(x)\)). The area of the circle, in terms of the Euclidean radius \(x\) is thus

\begin{align*} A \amp= \int_0^{2\pi}\int_0^x\frac{4r}{(1+r^2)^2}~dr~d\theta\\ \amp= \int_0^{2\pi}\int_1^{1+x^2}\frac{2}{u^2}~du~d\theta \tag{letting $u = 1 + r^2$}\\ \amp= \int_0^{2\pi}\bigg(2-\frac{2}{1+x^2}\bigg)~d\theta\\ \amp= 2\pi\bigg(2-\frac{2}{1+x^2}\bigg)\\ \amp= 2\pi\frac{2x^2}{1+x^2}\text{.} \end{align*}

To express the area in terms of the elliptic radius \(r\text{,}\) set \(x = \tan(r/2)\) so that

\begin{align*} A\amp= \frac{4\pi\tan^2(\frac{r}{2})}{1 + \tan^2(\frac{r}{2})}\\ \amp= \frac{4\pi\sin^2(\frac{r}{2})}{\cos^2(\frac{r}{2})+\sin^2(\frac{r}{2})}\tag{multiply top and bottom by $\cos^2(\frac{r}{2})$}\\ \amp= 4\pi\sin^2\left(\frac{r}{2}\right)\text{.} \end{align*}
